# 07 The Archive of Natural Phenomena & Human Perception

This module serves as the settlement's foundational "encyclopedia"—a synthesized archive of how the previous civilization perceived, measured, and pattern-recognized the natural world. It is designed to provide deep understanding without the need for unsustainable technology.

## 1. The Earth System (Lithosphere & Pedosphere)
### 1.1. Soil & Nutrient Cycling (Liebig's Law of the Minimum)
Understanding the "limiting factor" is critical for regenerative agriculture.
*   **The Law:** Plant growth is controlled not by the total amount of resources available, but by the **scarcest resource** (the weakest link).
*   **The Barrel Analogy:** Imagine a barrel with staves of different heights. The water (growth) can only rise to the level of the shortest stave (e.g., if you lack water, adding more nitrogen will not help).
*   **The Principle:** To improve yield, you must identify and fix the single most lacking factor first.

## 2. The Energetic System (Thermodynamics & Flow)
### 2.1. The Laws of Energy (Mechanical Summary)
Energy is the currency of the universe; it can be transferred but never created.
*   **The First Law (Conservation):** "You can't get something for nothing." Energy in = Energy out + Work done. You cannot create energy; you can only transform it from one state (e.g., fuel) to another (e.g., motion).
*   **The Second Law (Entropy):** "You can't even break even." No machine is 100% efficient. Every energy conversion loses some "useful" energy as waste heat. The universe naturally tends toward disorder (entropy).

## 3. The Biological Web (Biosphere & Ecology)
### 3.1. The 5 Major Phyla of the Animal Kingdom
Understanding the body plans and patterns of animal life:
1.  **Porifera (Sponges):** Cellular level; no true tissues. Filter feeders with a porous body.
2.  **Cnidaria (Jellyfish/Corals):** Tissue level; radial symmetry. Stinging cells and a single opening for both mouth and anus.
3.  **Mollusca (Snails/Octopuses):** Organ level; bilateral symmetry. Soft-bodied with a muscular foot and often a protective shell.
4.  **Arthropoda (Insects/Spiders):** Segmented bodies with a chitinous exoskeleton and jointed appendages. The most diverse phylum on Earth.
5.  **Chordata (Vertebrates):** Advanced organ systems with a dorsal nerve cord and (usually) a skeletal backbone. Includes humans and all major draft animals.
