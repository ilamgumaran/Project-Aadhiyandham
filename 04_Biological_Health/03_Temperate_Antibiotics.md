# The Temperate Antibiotic Kit: Preparation Guide

## 1. Why This Is Important
In an isolated settlement, a minor skin infection or respiratory illness can escalate into a critical failure. Without access to synthetic pharmaceuticals, we must rely on the biochemical power of plants. This module identifies five "foundational healers" that grow in temperate mountain environments and provides the logic for turning them into stable medical artifacts.

## 2. Expected Outcome
A modular pharmacy capable of managing minor bacterial, fungal, and viral threats, and a set of skills for sanitizing wounds and the respiratory tract.

---

## 3. How-To: Preparing the Arsenal

### Artifact 1: Garlic Extract (The Broad Spectrum)
*   **Active:** Allicin.
*   **Preparation:** **Must be crushed and raw.** The reaction that creates allicin occurs when the cell walls are broken and exposed to air. Wait 10 mins after crushing before use.
*   **Application:** Mix with honey for internal respiratory infections; apply as a poultice for skin fungal infections.

### Artifact 2: Thyme Oil / Tea (The Disinfectant)
*   **Active:** Thymol.
*   **Preparation:** Infuse 20g of fresh thyme in 200ml of hot water (not boiling) for 15 mins.
*   **Application:** Use as a gargle for sore throats; use the concentrated tea to wash surgical tools and wounds.

### Artifact 3: Echinacea Tincture (The Booster)
*   **Preparation:** Clean and chop the roots. Submerge in high-proof alcohol (Module 08) for 4 weeks. Strain.
*   **Application:** Take 5ml at the very first sign of a tickle in the throat or fatigue.

### Artifact 4: Sage Wash (The Oral Healer)
*   **Preparation:** Strong decoction (boil leaves for 10 mins).
*   **Application:** Essential for gingivitis and mouth ulcers common in high-stress/low-variety diets.

### Artifact 5: Plantain Poultice (The Field Dress)
*   **Active:** Aucubin.
*   **Preparation:** **"Spit Poultice."** Chew the clean leaves until they form a pulp.
*   **Application:** Apply directly to stings, bites, or minor cuts. The saliva enzymes and plant compounds immediately reduce swelling and prevent infection.

---

## 4. How-To: Basic Wound Sanitation (Step-by-Step)

1.  **Cleanse:** Flush the wound with boiled, cooled water.
2.  **Disinfect:** Apply a cooled Thyme infusion or a diluted Garlic honey.
3.  **Seal:** Use a clean linen cloth. If bleeding is heavy, use Yarrow (Module 02) to stop the flow.
4.  **Monitor:** Check for "Heat, Redness, and Swelling." If infection spreads, increase internal Garlic intake.

## 6. Maintenance Guide
*   **Rotation:** Harvest and dry these herbs every summer. Most lose their medicinal potency after 12-18 months.
*   **Storage:** Keep all medical artifacts in **Amber Glass** (Module 08) to protect the volatile oils from UV degradation.
