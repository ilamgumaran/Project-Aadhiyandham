# The Recycling Cliff: Strategic Resource Management

## 1. Why This Is Important
Most settlers will arrive with a "scavenger mindset," assuming the ruins of the past will provide tools forever. This is a trap. Entropy and corrosion are constant. This module provides the logic for identifying the "cliff edge"—the moment when salvage becomes a liability—and forces the settlement to prioritize primary synthesis (Module 03) early.

## 2. Expected Outcome
A strategic timeline for the settlement's transition to material sovereignty, ensuring no gap in capability as old-world steel and copper fail.

---

## 3. How-To: The Annual Materials Audit (Step-by-Step)

### Step 1: Inventory Categorization
Every year, categorize all metallic tools and structural components into three groups:
1.  **Stable:** Galvanized steel, Copper, Stainless steel (minimal rust).
2.  **Degrading:** Bare carbon steel with surface rust.
3.  **Critical Failure:** Pitted steel, crumbling joints, brittle tools.

### Step 2: Calculate the "Cliff"
Observe the rate of degradation. 
*   **Formula:** If a 1cm steel bar loses 1mm of thickness to rust in 1 year, your "Cliff" for that material is **5 years** (before it loses 50% structural integrity).
*   **Action:** You must have a functional Bloomery (Module 03) operational 2 years *before* the first cliff edge.

### Step 3: Prioritized Scavenging
Focus scavenging efforts on "High-Value Batteries" of material:
*   **The Copper Battery:** Electrical transformers, heavy industrial wiring, plumbing.
*   **The Carbon Steel Battery:** Vehicle leaf springs, industrial saw blades, railroad spikes.
*   **The Zinc Battery:** Galvanized roofing sheets (harvest for zinc to create brass/bronze).

---

## 4. How-To: Preserving the Legacy (The Stop-Gap)
Until the Bloomery is ready, use these methods to slow the cliff:
1.  **The Oil Bath:** Submerge high-carbon tools in rendered animal fat or linseed oil when not in use.
2.  **Charcoal Paint:** Mix crushed charcoal with pine resin and apply to structural steel to block oxygen.
