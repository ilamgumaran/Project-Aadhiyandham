# Bio-Security: Water Purification (Slow Sand Filter)

## 1. Why This Is Important
Pathogens (bacteria, viruses, parasites) in untreated water are the most immediate threat to any settlement. A **Slow Sand Filter (SSF)** is a "living" technology. Unlike a mechanical sieve, it uses a biological layer called a *Schmutzdecke* to consume harmful organisms. It requires no electricity, no chemical refills, and can last for years with basic maintenance.

## 2. Expected Outcome
A consistent supply of **2,000 Liters of safe drinking water per day**, sufficient for a pioneer group of 50 people (approx. 40L per person for drinking, cooking, and basic hygiene).

## 3. Materials Needed & Sourcing

| Material | Quantity | How to Find / Source |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **The Tank** | 1 (2.5m height) | Scavenge a large plastic/metal tank (e.g., 2,500L), or build a cylinder using stones and lime-clay mortar. |
| **Fine Sand** | ~1.5 cubic meters | Source from riverbanks or sandy deposits. **Must be washed** multiple times to remove silt/organic matter. Target size: 0.15–0.35mm. |
| **Gravel** | ~0.5 cubic meters | Source from riverbeds. Use two sizes: pea-gravel (6-12mm) and larger support gravel (20-40mm). |
| **Biochar** | ~0.4 cubic meters | Produced via pyrolysis (Module 03). Use hardwood charcoal crushed to pea-size. |
| **Piping** | ~5-10 meters | Scavenge PVC, copper, or lead (if properly lined). Use for the internal manifold and the exterior "riser" pipe. |

## 4. Assembly Instructions (Step-by-Step)

### Step 1: The Internal Manifold
At the very bottom of the tank, place a perforated pipe (the manifold) to collect the filtered water. Cover this pipe with the largest gravel to prevent the holes from clogging with sand.

### Step 2: Layering the Bed
Add materials in the following order (Bottom to Top):
1.  **Support Gravel (30cm):** Larger stones first, then pea-gravel.
2.  **Biochar (20cm):** This acts as a chemical sponge for odors and toxins.
3.  **Fine Sand (80cm):** The core of the filter. Level it carefully.
4.  **The Water Head (100cm):** Leave at least 1 meter of space above the sand for the "weight" of the water to provide gravity pressure.

### Step 3: The External Riser Pipe
**CRITICAL:** Connect the outlet pipe from the bottom manifold and run it up the *outside* of the tank. The exit point of this pipe must be **higher than the sand level**.
*Rationale:* This ensures the sand bed is always submerged, which is necessary to keep the biological layer (Schmutzdecke) alive.

### Step 4: The Ripening Period
Fill the tank with raw water. Do not drink the output yet.
1.  Allow water to flow slowly through the system.
2.  Wait **10-14 days**. During this time, a green/brown film will form on top of the sand.
3.  Test the water (if possible) or observe clarity. Once the film is established, the water is safe.

## 6. Maintenance Guide
*   **Constant Flow:** Never stop the water for more than a few hours. The bio-layer needs oxygen from moving water to stay alive.
*   **The "Scrape":** If flow slows down significantly, wait for the water to drain to just above the sand, then scrape off the top 1-2cm of sand/film. The filter will reset and ripen again in 2-3 days.
