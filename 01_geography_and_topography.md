# 01 Geography & The Ideal Habitat

This module defines the specific environmental and topographical criteria for a location that can support human life and its connected ecosystem with minimal energy inputs and maximum protection.

## 1. Topographical Resilience & Climate Buffering
### 1.1. Elevation & Drainage
*   **Sea Level Buffering:** A baseline of **at least 100 meters above sea level**.
*   **Gravity Drainage:** Land should naturally shed excess water.

## 2. Solar & Thermal Balance
### 2.1. Sunlight Access (The Biological Dose)
The site must provide enough direct solar radiation to maintain biological and psychological health.
*   **Circadian Alignment:** Minimum **10–30 minutes** of direct outdoor sunlight (minimum 2,500 lux) within the first hour of waking to reset the Suprachiasmatic Nucleus (SCN).
*   **Vitamin D Synthesis:** Direct skin exposure to midday sun (**10 AM – 3 PM**) for **10–30 minutes**, at least twice a week (requires a UV Index of 3+).

## 3. Water Sovereignty
### 3.1. Zero-Energy Water Movement (The Hydraulic Ram Pump)
If the primary water source is below the settlement, use a **Hydram** to move water uphill without electricity.
*   **The Principle:** Uses the "water hammer" effect—a pressure surge created by suddenly stopping a moving column of water.
*   **Design Ratios:** 
    *   **Drive Pipe:** 5–12 times the supply head height.
    *   **Delivery Height:** Can lift water 10–20 times higher than the source drop.
*   **Components:** Drive pipe, waste valve (clack), check valve, and a pressure vessel (air buffer) to ensure continuous flow.
