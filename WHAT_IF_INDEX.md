# The What-If Index (என்ன ஆனால்?)

**A searchable guide for the moment the plan stops working.**
Companions: [pressure_test_report.md](pressure_test_report.md) & [settling_pressure_test_report.md](settling_pressure_test_report.md) (the audits these scenarios came from) · [CONSEQUENCES_OF_DEVIATION.md](CONSEQUENCES_OF_DEVIATION.md) (what it costs to ignore a critical instruction, and which numbers are exact-or-you-die).

> **How to use this index.** Find your question (search the page for a keyword: *water, injured, occupied, checkpoint, bored, winter…*), read the first moves, then go to the deep module. And hold one thing in mind while you do: **this index does not think for you — it gets you to a starting point.** Every entry is a rehearsed hypothesis, not a script; your ground, your people, and your own tested judgment outrank this page the moment they disagree with it. That is not a disclaimer, it is the method of the whole manual: read → practice → observe → revise. The traveler who has rehearsed ten of these scenarios will handle the eleventh — the one this index missed — better than any book could specify. That traveler is the goal, not this list.

## D. The First Year

| What if… | First moves | Go deeper |
|:---|:---|:---|
| …the first crop underperforms badly? | It usually does (30–60% of plan is the historical norm). This is what seed diversity, wild-staple knowledge, and the food margin were for — deploy all three and adjust the second planting from the first's data. | [Pressure Test §5.3](pressure_test_report.md), [Emergency Nutrition](Outcome_2_Biological_Sovereignty/04_Food_and_Soil_Sovereignty/06_Emergency_Nutrition.md), [Heirloom Seed Banking](Outcome_2_Biological_Sovereignty/04_Food_and_Soil_Sovereignty/03_Heirloom_Seed_Banking.md) |
| …people start getting stomach illness? | Assume your own water/sanitation loop first — it is almost always the latrine, the hands, or the "clean" source. Re-verify the chain end to end; boil everything until found. | [Immediate Sanitation](Outcome_2_Biological_Sovereignty/03_Water_and_Sanitation/03_Immediate_Sanitation.md), [Thermophilic Sanitation Math](Outcome_2_Biological_Sovereignty/03_Water_and_Sanitation/06_Thermophilic_Sanitation_Math.md) |
| …the water is clear and tastes fine but a baby goes blue, or people sicken slowly over years? | Boiling and sand filters do **not** remove chemical poisons — nitrate (blue-baby), arsenic, metals — and boiling arsenic *concentrates* it. Switch infants/pregnancy to rainwater or a known-clean upstream source now; assess the source by where it's been and the local geology. | [Water Contaminant Testing](Outcome_2_Biological_Sovereignty/03_Water_and_Sanitation/07_Water_Contaminant_Testing_and_Remediation.md), [Consequences §4](CONSEQUENCES_OF_DEVIATION.md) |
| …stored grain/preserves look or smell slightly off — can we still eat them? | For four things, **no, and cooking won't fix it**: ergot (dark curved grains), mold/aflatoxin, any doubtful anaerobic preserve (botulism — never taste to check), and warm-held cooked greens. Discard the batch; the hunger of waste is survivable, these are not. | [Food Toxins & Storage Safety](Outcome_2_Biological_Sovereignty/04_Food_and_Soil_Sovereignty/09_Food_Toxins_and_Storage_Safety.md), [Consequences §6](CONSEQUENCES_OF_DEVIATION.md) |
| …by late winter people are tired, irritable, gums bleeding, a child not thriving? | Read it as a **deficiency alarm**, not morale: scurvy hits in 6–12 weeks of no vitamin C; the staple traps (pellagra from un-nixtamalized maize, beriberi from over-milled grain) punish monotony. Diversify now — needle tea, kraut, sprouts, whole grain, animal fat. | [Deficiency Diseases & Heat Illness](Outcome_2_Biological_Sovereignty/05_Health_and_the_Body/10_Deficiency_Diseases_and_Heat_Illness.md) |
| …a worker gets dizzy and confused in the heat? | The danger line is **sweating stops / confusion starts** = heat stroke, fatal in hours. Pull them, cool hard and immediately (water, wet-and-fan, cool packs to neck/armpits/groin). Heat exhaustion (still sweating) recovers with shade, water, rest. | [Deficiency Diseases & Heat Illness §4](Outcome_2_Biological_Sovereignty/05_Health_and_the_Body/10_Deficiency_Diseases_and_Heat_Illness.md) |
| …I'm not sure which instructions I must follow exactly vs. can adapt? | Learn the three tiers: **Law** (exact — pH 4.6, 55°C×15 days, 30 m setback, CO vent — measure, never round), **Craft** (a range you calibrate by a test), **Preference** (adapt freely). | …serious injury or a difficult birth happens here, without a hospital? | The trauma and midwifery protocols are the best zero-tech medicine available — and the manual owes you honesty: some events they cannot save. Prevention culture (sanitation, dental, no risky solo work) is where the real mortality reduction lives. | [Advanced Trauma Care](Outcome_2_Biological_Sovereignty/05_Health_and_the_Body/04_Advanced_Trauma_Care.md), [Midwifery and Childbirth](Outcome_2_Biological_Sovereignty/05_Health_and_the_Body/07_Midwifery_and_Childbirth.md), [Pressure Test §5.5](pressure_test_report.md) |

| …wildfire threatens the site? | The defensible space you cut in month one is the plan; ignition discipline and the fire-break ring are maintenance, not crisis response. | [Wildfire Defensible Space](Outcome_3_Perimeter_Defense/07_Passive_Defense/03_Wildfire_Defensible_Space.md) |
| …outsiders discover the settlement? | First contact is a protocol, not an improvisation: observation, graduated disclosure, bounded generosity — the settlement-side versions of the road doctrine. | [First Contact Protocols](Outcome_6_Flourishing_Civilization/14a_Inter_Settlement_Diplomacy/02_First_Contact_Protocols.md), [De-escalation](Outcome_3_Perimeter_Defense/07a_Active_Threat_Response/03_De_escalation_and_Non_Lethal_Deterrence.md) |

## The scenario this index missed

There will be one. When you meet it: stop, breathe once the long way out, and run the method every module here runs — *what do I observe (not assume)? what does it threaten first (water > warmth > safety > food)? what's the cheapest reversible move?* Make that move, observe again, and write down what happened. Then — when there is time — add the scenario to this index for the next traveler. That final step is not optional housekeeping; it is how a manual stays alive, and how you repay the strangers who wrote the entries that got you this far.
