**Alignment:** Outcome 2: Absolute Biological Sovereignty

> **Caution — the humblest medicine is the strongest:** No antibiotic in the settlement's kit prevents as much death as a bar of soap and clean bedding. The diseases that emptied pre-industrial cities and armies — typhus, plague, dysentery, cholera — spread through dirty hands, lousy clothing, and flea-ridden bedding far more than through anything dramatic. The settlement rebuilds exactly those conditions, and its defense is not heroic medicine but a boring, relentless daily discipline. Let hygiene lapse and you invite the epidemics the [quarantine module](11_Epidemic_and_Quarantine_Protocol.md) can only react to; keep it and most of those outbreaks simply never start.

## 1. Why This Is Important

The single most powerful public-health intervention in history is not a drug; it is **hand-washing with soap and the general cleanliness of bodies, clothing, and living space.** Fecal-oral diseases (dysentery, cholera, typhoid, hepatitis, worms) travel on unwashed hands; louse-borne typhus and flea-borne plague travel on unwashed clothing and bedding and on the rodents that share them. These are not exotic threats — they are the ordinary killers of any crowded, dirty, low-technology settlement, and history's death tolls from them dwarf those from war.

The good news mirrors the [epidemic module's](11_Epidemic_and_Quarantine_Protocol.md) lesson: you defeat these by *breaking the chain*, and the chain-breaks here are cheap, learnable daily habits built on the settlement's own [soap](../../Outcome_5_Decadal_Resilience/09_The_Mechanics/07_Foundational_Chemistry.md), water, and sun. This module is the daily discipline that keeps the settlement out of the epidemic module.

## 2. Expected Outcome

A settlement culture in which hand-washing with soap is automatic (before food and after the latrine), bodies and living spaces are kept clean, clothing and bedding are regularly laundered and sun-dried, and the parasites that carry serious disease — lice, fleas, ticks, mites, worms, mosquitoes — are actively controlled. The measure of success is the outbreaks that never happen.

## 3. Personal Hygiene: The Daily Chain-Breaks

*   **Hand-washing with soap — the keystone.** Before handling food, after the latrine, after handling the sick or animals or the dead. This single act blocks the fecal-oral route more than anything else the settlement does; it is why [soap-making](../../Outcome_5_Decadal_Resilience/09_The_Mechanics/07_Foundational_Chemistry.md) sits on the critical path and why a handwashing station belongs at every latrine (see [sanitation](../03_Water_and_Sanitation/03_Immediate_Sanitation.md)).
*   **Bathing.** Regular washing of the body with soap and water removes the sweat, dirt, and skin parasites that breed disease and infection; it also keeps skin intact (broken, dirty skin is an infection route). It need not be daily or hot, but it must be regular.
*   **Oral hygiene.** Covered in full by [zero-tech dentistry](05_Zero_Tech_Dentistry.md) — a dental abscess is a settlement killer, and cleaning teeth is its prevention.
*   **Wound cleanliness.** Clean minor wounds promptly (the [antibiotic kit](03_Temperate_Antibiotic_Kit.md) and the 48-hour escalation rule govern the rest); a clean wound rarely becomes the sepsis a dirty one does.

## 4. Laundry: Not Vanity, But Vector Control

Washing clothing and bedding is a *disease-control* operation, not a cosmetic one, because the parasites that carry typhus and plague *live in cloth*:

*   **Wash with soap and hot water.** Heat and soap kill the lice, fleas, mites, and their eggs that hide in clothing seams and bedding — this is the direct break in the louse-borne-typhus and flea-borne-plague chains. Boiling infested clothing and bedding is the surest method when disease is present.
*   **Sun-dry.** Sunlight's UV is a free disinfectant; drying laundry in strong sun kills lingering organisms and airs the parasites out. Bedding and clothing aired and sunned regularly stay far cleaner than those kept perpetually indoors.
*   **The cost is real.** Laundry is heavy labor and consumes water, soap, and fuel (for hot water); the settlement must *budget* for it rather than let it slide, because the temptation under a heavy workload is to skip it — and skipped laundry is how a louse population, and then typhus, takes hold. Prioritize the laundering of the sick's and the infested's items above all.

## 5. Parasite and Vector Control

Each disease vector has its own break; the settlement should know the main ones:

*   **Body and head lice → typhus (and relapsing fever).** Body lice live in *clothing*, not on the body, and carry epidemic typhus — a historic mass killer of crowded, unwashed populations (prisons, armies, refugee columns). The defense is *laundry*: regular hot-washing/boiling of clothes and bedding, plus combing and body hygiene. In an infestation, treating clothing and bedding matters as much as treating the body.
*   **Fleas → plague.** Fleas ride rodents, so **rodent control is plague control**: keep [food stores rodent-proof](../04_Food_and_Soil_Sovereignty/08_Refugia_Pantry_Physics.md), deny rodents harborage near living areas, and keep animals and bedding clean. A rodent die-off near a settlement is a plague warning sign.
*   **Ticks → several serious diseases.** Clear tall vegetation around living and working areas, check bodies after being in brush, and let [poultry — especially guinea fowl](../06_Ecological_Harmony/09_Small_Livestock_Poultry_and_Rabbits.md) — patrol for ticks (they eat them voraciously).
*   **Mites → scabies.** Spread by close contact and shared bedding; controlled by hygiene and by treating clothing and bedding, same as lice.
*   **Mosquitoes → malaria and other fevers.** Break the breeding chain: **eliminate standing water** near the settlement (the pools, containers, and wet spots where mosquitoes breed), screen or net sleeping areas where mosquito disease is present, and site living areas away from stagnant water. Note the tension with the [fish pond](../06_Ecological_Harmony/08_Fishing_and_Aquaculture.md) — a stocked pond's fish eat mosquito larvae, so *managed* water is fine; it is *stagnant, unmanaged* water that breeds disease.
*   **Intestinal worms → chronic illness and malnutrition.** Broken by sanitation ([latrines](../03_Water_and_Sanitation/03_Immediate_Sanitation.md)), hand-washing, wearing footwear (some worms enter through the feet from contaminated soil), and cooking meat and fish thoroughly ([food safety](../04_Food_and_Soil_Sovereignty/09_Food_Toxins_and_Storage_Safety.md), [fish parasites](../06_Ecological_Harmony/08_Fishing_and_Aquaculture.md)).

## 6. Common Failure Modes

*   **Hand-washing lapses.** Skipping soap before food or after the latrine. **[Tier L] Consequence:** the fecal-oral route reopens; dysentery/cholera/worms spread. **Prevention:** handwashing stations everywhere, soap always available, the habit taught from childhood as non-negotiable.
*   **Skipping laundry under workload.** Letting clothing and bedding go unwashed because labor is scarce. **[Tier L] Consequence:** lice and fleas establish, and with them the risk of typhus and plague — the historic camp-and-siege killers. **Prevention:** budget laundry as essential labor; hot-wash and sun-dry regularly; prioritize the sick's items.
*   **Rodents in the stores.** Tolerating rats and mice near food and living areas. **[Tier L] Consequence:** flea-borne plague risk plus food loss and contamination. **Prevention:** rodent-proof storage; deny harborage; treat a rodent die-off as a warning.
*   **Standing water near living areas.** Pools and containers breeding mosquitoes. **[Tier C→L] Consequence:** mosquito-borne fevers where those diseases exist. **Prevention:** drain unmanaged standing water; net sleeping areas; keep water either moving or stocked with fish.
*   **Hygiene collapse during crisis.** Letting cleanliness slide exactly when crowded, stressed, or sick. **[Tier L] Consequence:** crisis conditions plus poor hygiene is the classic recipe for a secondary epidemic on top of the first problem. **Prevention:** hold hygiene discipline *hardest* during crises; it is cheapest to maintain and costliest to lose then.

## 7. Vocabulary of the Foundation

*   **Body lice:** Clothing-dwelling lice that carry epidemic typhus; controlled by laundering, not just bathing.
*   **Delousing:** Ridding people and their clothing/bedding of lice, chiefly by hot-washing and hygiene.
*   **Fecal-oral route:** The path by which gut pathogens reach a new mouth (hands, water, food, flies); broken chiefly by hand-washing and sanitation.
*   **Harborage:** Shelter that lets pests (especially rodents) live near people; denying it is core to vector control.
*   **Vector:** An organism (louse, flea, tick, mosquito, rodent) that carries disease between hosts; each has its own control.

## 8. Practical Implementation Guide for Beginners

### 8.1. Step-by-Step Action Plan
*   **Preparation:** Ensure a steady [soap](../../Outcome_5_Decadal_Resilience/09_The_Mechanics/07_Foundational_Chemistry.md) supply and handwashing stations; set up a laundry area with hot-water capacity and sun-drying lines.
*   **Execution:** Make hand-washing automatic; launder and sun-dry clothing and bedding on a schedule; control rodents and standing water; check for ticks and lice.
*   **Review:** Watch for the warning signs (lice, rodent die-offs, clusters of gut illness) and tighten the relevant break immediately.

### 8.2. Consequence of Deviation & Precision Tier
*   **[Tier L — Law] Hand-washing and laundry discipline lapse** → the classic epidemics (dysentery, typhus, plague) find their opening. *These daily habits are frontline medicine; hold them hardest in crisis.*
*   **[Tier L — Law] Rodents in stores / standing water at living areas** → plague and mosquito-borne disease risk. *Rodent-proof and drain.*
*   **[Tier C — Craft] Bathing/laundry frequency** → calibrate to climate, water, and workload — but never to zero.

### 8.3. Community & Decadal Flourishing
*   **Apprenticeship:** Hygiene is culture, not a skill held by one person; it must be taught to every child as automatic habit and *maintained* across good decades, because a settlement that has forgotten why it washes and launders will relax exactly the defenses that kept it out of the epidemic module — the [dogma/forgotten-reasoning](../../Outcome_5_Decadal_Resilience/11_The_Next_Generation/08_The_Scientific_Method.md) failure in public-health form.
