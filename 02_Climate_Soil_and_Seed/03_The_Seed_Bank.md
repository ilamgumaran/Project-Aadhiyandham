# The Seed Bank: Planting and Saving Guide

## 1. Why This Is Important
Modern seeds are often hybrids that do not "breed true," meaning their offspring will be weak or sterile. In Aadhiyandham, we exclusively use **Open-Pollinated Heirloom Seeds**. These seeds carry the genetic history of civilization and allow the settlement to become biologically independent. By saving seeds every year, you adapt your crops to your specific mountain microclimate.

## 2. Expected Outcome
A permanent, locally-adapted source of high-density calories that requires zero external seed purchases or inputs.

---

## 3. How-To: Managing the Primary Staples

### Staple 1: Potatoes (German Butterball / Kennebec)
*   **Planting:** Cut a "seed potato" into pieces with at least two "eyes" (sprouts). Bury 15cm deep in your Terra Preta soil.
*   **Maintenance:** "Hill" the plants by pulling soil up around the stem as they grow to encourage more tubers.
*   **Saving:** Keep the best, disease-free tubers from the harvest in a cool, dark, and well-ventilated "root cellar." Do not wash them before storage.

### Staple 2: Corn (Glass Gem / Hickory King Flint)
*   **Planting:** Plant in blocks (e.g., 4x4 rows) rather than single lines to ensure wind pollination.
*   **Maintenance:** Interplant with beans (for nitrogen) and squash (for ground cover)—the **Three Sisters** method.
*   **Saving:** Leave the best ears on the stalk until the husks are dry and the kernels are hard. Store in a rodent-proof bin.

### Staple 3: Quinoa & Amaranth
*   **Planting:** Tiny seeds; sow on the surface and lightly press in.
*   **Saving:** Wait until the seed heads are dry and the seeds feel like "flint." Rub the heads over a bucket and winnow (blow away the chaff) in a light breeze.

---

## 4. How-To: The Art of Seed Saving (General)

### Step 1: Isolation
To keep varieties pure, plant different varieties of the same species (e.g., two types of corn) at least 500 meters apart, or time their flowering so they do not overlap.

### Step 2: Selection
**CRITICAL:** Do not save seeds from the first plant to ripen (it may be weak) or the last. Save seeds from the **strongest, healthiest, and most resilient** plants in the middle of the season.

### Step 3: Processing
*   **Dry Seeds (Corn/Beans):** Allow to dry completely on the plant.
*   **Wet Seeds (Tomatoes/Squash):** Scoop seeds out, ferment in water for 2 days to remove the gel coating, then dry on a screen.

### Step 4: Storage
Store all seeds in airtight glass jars. Keep them in the **darkest and coolest** part of your shelter. Label with the Variety and the Year.
