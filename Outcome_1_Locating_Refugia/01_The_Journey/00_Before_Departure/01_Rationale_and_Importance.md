**Alignment:** Outcome 1: Locating and Connecting Optimal Refugia

> **Caution — the manual's largest silent assumption:** Every other section of this manual begins after three things already exist: a *group* of aligned, capable people; the *skills* to execute the plans; and a *decision* to actually go. None of the three is automatic, and the honest audit of this project found that all three were assumed rather than built. A perfect journey plan executed by strangers who have never lit a fire, and who left three weeks too late, fails before the first kilometre. This is Phase Zero: the work that must happen *before* the journey, in the months and years while normal life still runs.

## 1. Why This Section Exists

The journey modules answer "how do we get there?" The settling modules answer "how do we live once we arrive?" Between the reader's present life and the first step of the journey sits a gap the manual never addressed — and it is the gap where most attempts die, quietly, before they begin. Three questions live in that gap:

1.  **Who is going?** The plan assumes a founding party of aligned, skill-diverse, committed people. Almost no reader has one. Assembling and testing that group — its trust, its values, its skills, its way of deciding — is the true first task, and it is harder and slower than any construction project in this manual.
2.  **Can they actually do any of this?** Reading how to build a bio-sand filter is not the same as having built one. A settlement founded by people who have only *read* the manual will discover, at the worst possible moment, that reading is not skill. The critical few skills must be practiced with the hands, in advance, while failure is survivable.
3.  **When do they leave?** Under any scenario where departure is triggered by deteriorating conditions, the hardest decision is *when* — and human psychology guarantees the answer will be "too late" unless the triggers are decided in advance, in calm, before the pressure distorts judgment.

Phase Zero is the answer to all three, and it has a property the rest of the manual does not: **it must be done now, while the world still works** — while there are hardware stores, libraries, teachers, medical care, and time to fail and try again. The window for Phase Zero is exactly the window that closes when the journey becomes necessary. A group that waits until it *must* go to begin Phase Zero has already lost the only conditions under which Phase Zero is easy.

## 2. The S0 Reframe: This Is Not Doom Prep

The [master audit](../../../pressure_test_report.md) found that the manual reads as if collapse were assumed, when in fact the most likely future is that society keeps functioning — and Phase Zero is precisely where that reframe becomes practical. **Every task in this section is worth doing under completely normal conditions:**

*   Assembling a trusted, skilled, values-aligned group is the foundation of any intentional community, cooperative, or land project — legal, ordinary, and life-enriching in a world that never ends.
*   Learning to grow food, preserve it, render first aid, build simply, and steady your own mind makes you more capable and less anxious *today*, collapse or no collapse.
*   Deciding your own thresholds — what would make you change your life, and when — is simply living deliberately.

Phase Zero, done in S0, is not a bunker. It is a **better, more capable, more connected life that also happens to be resilient.** That framing matters, because a group that treats Phase Zero as morbid emergency prep will do it joylessly and badly; a group that treats it as building a richer life will actually do it, and be ready if the richer life is ever interrupted.

Each module is a starting hypothesis, not a script — the same stance as everywhere in this manual ([The Scientific Method](../../../Outcome_5_Decadal_Resilience/11_The_Next_Generation/08_The_Scientific_Method.md)). The one thing Phase Zero cannot tolerate is *postponement*: unlike a wall you can build later, the group, the skills, and the decision framework can only be built *before*, and the reader who takes one thing from this section should take that.

## 4. Expected Outcome

A reader who works through Phase Zero will have: a real, tested founding group (or the honest knowledge that they do not yet have one, and a plan to build it); demonstrated competence in the critical few skills, practiced with the hands; and a written, pre-agreed departure-trigger ladder that removes the fatal in-the-moment "not yet." These are the three preconditions every later module silently assumes — made explicit, and built while they still can be.

## 5. Vocabulary of the Foundation

*   **Phase Zero:** The preparation window before the journey — group, skills, decision — that can only be built while normal life still runs.
*   **The critical few:** The small set of skills that must be hand-practiced before departure because failing to have them is lethal and un-improvisable; Module 03 lists them.
*   **The founding group:** The aligned, skill-diverse, committed party the rest of the manual assumes exists; Module 02 builds it.
*   **The trigger ladder:** The pre-committed sequence of indicators (watch → prepare → go) that decides departure timing before pressure distorts judgment; Module 04 builds it.
