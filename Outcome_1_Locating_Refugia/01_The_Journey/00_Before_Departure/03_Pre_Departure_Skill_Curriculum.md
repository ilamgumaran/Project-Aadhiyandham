**Alignment:** Outcome 1: Locating and Connecting Optimal Refugia

> **Caution — reading is not skill:** This manual contains hundreds of procedures. A person who has read all of them and practiced none has a library, not a capability. Under stress, the prefrontal cortex that reads instructions goes offline; what remains is what your hands already know. The gap between "I read how to do this" and "I have done this" is measured, in an emergency, in lives. This module does not add new knowledge — it tells you *which* of the manual's skills you must convert from reading into doing, *before* departure, while failure is a lesson and not a funeral.

## 1. Why This Is Important

The whole manual is the curriculum for a civilization; no group can master it before departure. But a small subset is different: skills where the *first* failure is catastrophic, where there is no time to learn on-site, and where reading provably does not transfer to doing. These are the **critical few** — and the audit found the manual named them nowhere as a prioritized, do-this-first program. A settlement founded by people fluent in theory and clumsy in practice discovers the gap at the worst moment: the fire that will not light in the rain, the wound no one has actually packed, the filter no one has actually built and waited out.

The purpose of a curriculum is triage of your limited practice time. You cannot hand-practice everything; you *must* hand-practice the critical few. This module ranks them by a single brutal criterion: **how bad is your first failure, and how soon does it come?**

## 2. Expected Outcome

Every founding member has personally, with their hands, done the Tier-1 skills below to a standard of competence — not read them, not watched them, *done* them, more than once, including once in bad conditions. The group has two-deep coverage ([Group Formation](02_Group_Formation.md)) on the life-critical ones. A logged record of what each person can actually do closes the [skill audit](02_Group_Formation.md).

## 3. The Practice Principle

*   **Practice to failure, at home, now.** Deliberately let the fire go out, the filter clog, the bread burn, the splint slip — while a mistake costs an evening, not a life. The failures are the curriculum; a skill you have only succeeded at in perfect conditions you do not yet own.
*   **Practice in bad conditions.** Light the fire in the rain, pack the wound by dim light, navigate in fog. The refugium will not offer ideal conditions, so ideal-condition competence is a false comfort.
*   **The transfer test.** You own a skill when you can do it *without the book*, tired, in poor conditions, and teach it to someone else. Until then you have read it.

## 6. Tier 3 — The Decadal Skills (learn on-site, from the archive)

The recycling-cliff crafts — bloomery iron, ceramics, lime, textiles, glass — are essential but *not* first-failure-lethal, and are impractical to master in advance. Carry them in the [Archive](../../../Outcome_5_Decadal_Resilience/12_The_Archive/01_Rationale_and_Importance.md), assign apprentices, and learn them across the years-long runway before scavenged materials fail ([Recycling Cliff](../../../Outcome_5_Decadal_Resilience/10_The_Society/02_Recycling_Cliff.md)). Trying to pre-master Tier 3 at the expense of Tier 1 is a triage error: it trades the skills that kill you in week one for skills you have years to acquire.

```text
   THE TRIAGE OF PRACTICE TIME
   ===========================
   first failure LETHAL + SOON   ->  TIER 1  practice to mastery NOW
   settlement-threatening, slower ->  TIER 2  practice before winter
   essential but years of runway  ->  TIER 3  learn on-site from archive

   Spending Tier-1 time on Tier-3 skills is how well-read groups
   die of the basics.
```

## 7. Common Failure Modes

*   **The library delusion.** Believing that having read (or carried) the manual equals capability. **[Tier L] Consequence:** the critical skill fails on first real attempt, when it counts, with no evening-at-home to try again. **Prevention:** the transfer test — do it without the book, tired, in bad conditions, before departure.
*   **Practicing only in good conditions.** Fires lit on dry sunny afternoons; wounds packed in good light. **[Tier C] Consequence:** the skill evaporates in the rain and the dark, which is when it is needed. **Prevention:** deliberately practice in bad conditions.
*   **Tier inversion.** Pouring practice into romantic Tier-3 crafts (forging!) while Tier-1 basics stay unpracticed. **[Tier L] Consequence:** death by a week-one basic the group never drilled. **Prevention:** the triage — Tier 1 to mastery first, always.
*   **No two-deep on life-critical skills.** Only one person can really stop a bleed or run the filter. **[Tier L] Consequence:** that person's loss removes the capability. **Prevention:** train backups before departure ([Group Formation](02_Group_Formation.md) audit).

## 8. Vocabulary of the Foundation

*   **Practice to failure:** Deliberately failing a skill at home to learn its real edges while the cost is trivial.
*   **The critical few:** The small set of skills whose first failure is fast and lethal and where reading does not transfer; practiced to mastery before departure.
*   **The transfer test:** The standard of true skill — done without the book, tired, in poor conditions, and teachable to another.
*   **Tier inversion:** The triage error of practicing distant Tier-3 crafts at the expense of week-one Tier-1 survival skills.

## 9. Practical Implementation Guide for Beginners

### 9.1. Step-by-Step Action Plan
*   **Preparation:** Print the Tier-1 list; assign each member the ones they lack; gather the (cheap, home-available) tools of each.
*   **Execution:** Practice each Tier-1 skill repeatedly, to failure, in bad conditions, until it passes the transfer test; log who can do what; then work Tier 2.
*   **Review:** Re-test annually — unpracticed skills decay. Fold results into the [Group Formation](02_Group_Formation.md) skill audit.

### 9.2. Consequence of Deviation & Precision Tier
*   **[Tier L — Law] Depart with Tier-1 skills only read, not done** → first-failure death at a basic. *The transfer test is the only proof; the book is not a substitute.*
*   **[Tier L — Law] Invert the tiers** → drilled the forge, never the fire. *Tier 1 to mastery before Tier 3, without exception.*
*   **[Tier C — Craft] How much Tier-2/3 to pre-learn** → calibrate to your runway and teachers; more is better but not at Tier-1's expense.

### 9.3. Community & Decadal Flourishing
*   **Apprenticeship:** This curriculum *is* the master/apprentice doctrine begun before departure. Every skill logged with two names is a single point of failure already prevented.
