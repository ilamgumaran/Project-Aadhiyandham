# Salt-Spray Management: Biological Resilience

**Alignment:** Outcome 1: Locating and Connecting Optimal Refugia

Living in a "Blue Refugia" (Coastal or Island) introduces the challenge of salinity. Salt spray is a corrosive force that affects both the biology of plants and the longevity of tools.

---

## 1. The Biological Impact of Salinity
Salt dehydrates plant tissues and alters soil chemistry, making it impossible for standard heirloom seeds to flourish near the shoreline.

### 1.1. The Salt-Belt Buffer
*   **Strategy:** Design your settlement in "Belts" moving away from the shore.
*   **Belt 1 (Frontline):** Plant salt-tolerant species like Seakale, Bayberry, or Beach Plum. These act as physical "Aerosol Filters," catching the salt spray before it reaches the interior.
*   **Belt 2 (Intermediate):** Salt-resistant windbreaks (evergreens).
*   **Belt 3 (The Core):** Your primary agriculture and housing, protected by the first two belts.

---

## 2. Technical Longevity: Corrosive Defense
Salt air accelerates the oxidation (rusting) of metals.

### 2.1. The Fat and Oil Protocol
In a coastal refugia, every metal tool (axes, knives, hinges) must be permanently coated in a thin layer of animal fat or plant oil (Module 05.05). Scavenged steel will reach the "Recycling Cliff" 50% faster in a salt environment without this maintenance.

### 2.2. Utilizing Ceramics
Because clay is immune to salt corrosion, coastal settlements should prioritize **Ceramic Hardware** (door handles, storage vessels, water pipes) over metal where possible.

---

## 3. 🔬 Scientific Validation: Halophytes
Research into **Halophytes** (salt-tolerant plants) shows they utilize specialized cells to sequester salt. By integrating these species into the perimeter, we create a "Biological Shield" that allows a coastal refugia to maintain high agricultural yields just meters from the ocean.

---
## 4. 4.Practical Implementation Guide for Beginners

### 4.1. Step-by-Step Action Plan
*   **Phase 1: Soil Testing:** Perform the "Indicator Plant" check (Module 02.06) to identify existing salt-tolerant species on your land.
*   **Phase 2: Buffer Planting:** Begin planting the "Frontline Belt" immediately upon arrival.
*   **Phase 3: Metal Audit:** Coat every piece of scavenged metal in beeswax or oil.

### 4.2. Troubleshooting & Failure Modes
*   **Leaf Burn:** If your core crops show brown, crispy edges, your salt-belt is too thin. Immediately build a temporary "Dead-Hedge" (Module 03.05) using brush to catch the spray until the living belt grows.
