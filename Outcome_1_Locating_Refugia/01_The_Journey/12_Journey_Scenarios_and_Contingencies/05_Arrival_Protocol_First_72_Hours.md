**Alignment:** Outcome 1: Locating and Connecting Optimal Refugia

> **Caution:** The most dangerous hours of the entire journey are the last ones. The party is at maximum fatigue, minimum supplies, and maximum emotional momentum — every instinct says *we're here, put the packs down.* This protocol exists to spend 2–4 more careful hours before that moment, because the errors made in them (committing to an occupied site, drinking untested water, sleeping without a perimeter) are the ones that cannot be walked back.

## 1. Why This Is Important

The [Transit Guide](../06_Transit_Guide_from_Georgia_USA.md) ends with "arrive before nightfall and establish a minimal perimeter." This module unpacks that sentence into a 72-hour discipline, because arrival is not an event — it is a *transition between operating systems*. On the journey, safety came from movement and concealment; at the site, it will come from position and preparation. The transition has a gap, and the gap has a shape:

*   **Hour 0–4:** the party knows nothing about the site's current state (occupation, water, hazards) and is too tired to learn fast.
*   **Day 1–3:** the party is present but has no perimeter, no tested water, no shelter, and no routine — maximally visible, minimally established.

Three questions, answered in order, close the gap: *Is it safe to commit?* (verification), *What kills us first?* (the priority ladder), and *How do we hold it while we build?* (the interim perimeter). Everything here is the 72-hour bridge into [The Site](../../02_The_Site/01_Rationale_and_Importance.md), [Water and Sanitation](../../../Outcome_2_Biological_Sovereignty/03_Water_and_Sanitation/01_Rationale_and_Importance.md), and [Passive Defense](../../../Outcome_3_Perimeter_Defense/07_Passive_Defense/01_Rationale_and_Importance.md) — which take over from hour 72.

## 2. Expected Outcome

At +4 hours: a verified-unoccupied site, or a clean decision to move to the fallback. At nightfall day one: tested water source, rain/wind shelter for the vulnerable, noise perimeter on the two likeliest approaches, watch or concealment plan running. At 72 hours: latrine dug and in use, water production routine, site survey begun, and the party sleeping on a rhythm. Nothing permanent is built in these 72 hours — *deliberately*.

## 3. Phase One — Verification Before Commitment (Hour 0–4)

The party halts **short of the site** — inside cover, 500 m–1 km out, at the last water before the objective. This is the **overwatch halt**. The packs come off here (rest is real work in this phase), and a two-person scout element (see the split rules in [Party Composition Playbooks](02_Party_Composition_Playbooks.md)) verifies the site while the party recovers.

```text
ARRIVAL DECISION SEQUENCE
=========================
Overwatch halt (500m-1km out, in cover, at water)
   |
   |--> LOOK (30-60 min, from high ground if any):
   |     smoke? structures? cut wood? paths worn in grass?
   |     birds flushing? dogs? laundry? crops? graves?
   |
   |--> LISTEN (part of same halt): voices, axes, animals
   |
   +--> SCOUT LOOP (2 persons, light, 1-3 hrs):
         perimeter walk at 200m offset, then close spiral
         checking: occupation sign (fresh ash, tracks, waste),
         water source state, hazard changes (deadfall, flood
         scars, burn sign) vs. the site dossier
   |
   RESULT?
   |-- UNOCCUPIED, matches dossier --> COMMIT (Phase Two)
   |-- SIGN OF PRIOR USE, now cold --> COMMIT CAUTIOUSLY:
   |     assume return visits; watch posture for week one
   |-- OCCUPIED --> DO NOT CONTEST. Withdraw unseen to
   |     overwatch. Options in order: (a) fallback site from
   |     the atlas dossier; (b) observed, open approach and
   |     parley IF the occupiers' sign suggests a small,
   |     settled, non-predatory group AND your party can
   |     offer skills/seed — arrivals negotiate from weakness,
   |     so parley is a choice, never a default; (c) wide
   |     detour and long alternate. Never camp adjacent
   |     "to wait and see" — proximity without introduction
   |     reads as threat everywhere on earth.
   +-- SITE DEGRADED (water failed, burn, flood) --> fallback
         dossier decision at the overwatch halt, with daylight
         budget in hand, not at dusk inside the ruin
```

Every refugium dossier in the [Candidate Atlas](../../03_Candidate_Atlas/) names conditions the site was chosen for; the scout's job is to falsify the dossier, not admire the destination. The fallback site — there must always be one, chosen before departure — is what makes "do not contest" psychologically possible. A party with no fallback will talk itself into contesting or camping adjacent, because the alternative is admitting the journey has no end yet.

## 4. Phase Two — The Priority Ladder (Day One)

Commitment made, the order of work is set by the physiology of dying, not by ambition. The rule of threes (≈3 hours unprotected in harsh exposure, ≈3 days without water, ≈3 weeks without food) gives the ladder; fatigue management threads through it because exhausted people execute all of it badly.

1.  **Water — verify, then produce (first hour).** Locate the dossier's source. Drink *nothing* untreated: clear is not clean, and a waterborne illness in week one is the single most likely lethal event of the entire settlement project (see [Rationale: Water and Sanitation](../../../Outcome_2_Biological_Sovereignty/03_Water_and_Sanitation/01_Rationale_and_Importance.md)). Boil is the day-one method — rolling boil, done (bio-sand filtration is a week-two build, not a day-one one). Assign the Quartermaster a standing production loop: fetch, boil, cool, cover.
2.  **Shelter — the vulnerable first (hours 1–4).** Tarp/lean-to for children and elders, backs to the wind, off the cold ground (brush mat). The able sleep rough one more night without harm; hypothermia in the young and old is the day-one exposure risk even in temperate climates when rain arrives on tired bodies.
3.  **The interim perimeter (before dark — Section 5).**
4.  **Fire discipline decision (dusk).** In verified-empty country: a small, screened cook fire at dusk, out after. Anywhere the scout found human sign: the dusk-camp doctrine from [Low-Profile Travel](04_Low_Profile_Travel_and_Encounter_Protocols.md) still governs — cook, move, sleep dark — until week-one observation says otherwise.
5.  **Food — last.** Ration from packs. Nobody forages, hunts, or harvests anything on day one; misidentified plants and injuries-while-tired are day-one classics, and the calorie math never justifies them.

**The fatigue override:** whatever remains undone at dusk stays undone. A half-built noise perimeter plus six hours of sleep beats a complete perimeter guarded by people hallucinating with exhaustion. Day two exists.

## 5. Phase Three — Holding While You Build (the Interim Perimeter)

Nothing built in 72 hours will stop a determined intrusion; that is not the goal. The interim perimeter has exactly two jobs: **make approach audible** and **make the camp forgettable**. Passive-first, in build order:

*   **Concealment siting (zero cost).** The first-night camp sits *off* the site's eventual center: tucked at the tree-line edge or terrace lip the [Initial Site Layout](../../02_The_Site/07_Initial_Site_Layout.md) will later designate, not proud in the open middle. No structure breaks a skyline. The site should read as empty for as long as possible while routines establish.
*   **Noise ground (first evening, 30 minutes).** On the two likeliest approach lines (the scout names them): dragged dry brush, dead branches, gravel scatter across a 3–5 m band. A free, weatherproof alarm that works while everyone sleeps — the transit-camp trick, now permanent (see [Acoustic Sovereignty](../../../Outcome_3_Perimeter_Defense/07_Passive_Defense/04_Acoustic_Sovereignty.md) for the mature version).
*   **Watch or concealment — by party math.** Four or more able adults: single sitting watch, 2-hour turns, positioned to hear the noise ground, not to fight — the watch's only weapon is waking everyone. Fewer than four: no watch is sustainable; sleep concealed and cold instead (the solo doctrine, scaled). A watch bill that collapses in three days is worse than none — it trains the party to trust a guard that isn't there.
*   **The rally point, re-used.** First evening, name the on-site rally point and the fallback (the overwatch halt is the natural one — known, watered, in cover). If the perimeter wakes the camp: dependents and Guardian move to rally, ables converge on the noise *with light and voice* — nearly every night alarm is an animal, and the response doctrine for both is presence and noise, not stealth (see [Large Predator Management](../../../Outcome_3_Perimeter_Defense/07b_Animal_Coexistence_Protocols/02_Large_Predator_Management.md)).
*   **What NOT to build yet.** No thorn barriers, no ditches, no visible fortification in week one. They cost calories the party doesn't have, advertise permanence and value to any observer, and — built before the site survey — are usually in the wrong place. The mature perimeter follows the layout, not the other way around ([Passive Perimeter Defense](../../../Outcome_3_Perimeter_Defense/07_Passive_Defense/02_Passive_Perimeter_Defense.md) takes over in week two+).

## 6. Hours 24–72 — Establishing the Rhythm

*   **Day 2 morning: the latrine.** A pit latrine 60+ m downhill/downstream of the water source, before any other construction (see [Immediate Sanitation](../../../Outcome_2_Biological_Sovereignty/03_Water_and_Sanitation/03_Immediate_Sanitation.md)). Fecal-oral contamination of the camp's own water is the settlement-killer of historical record; it is defeated by a hole dug on day two.
*   **Day 2–3: the site survey walk.** The full-party version of the scout loop, unhurried: confirm the dossier's water math, mark the layout zones, flag hazards (widow-maker limbs over any sleep site, flood lines, animal trails through camp). This walk is where [The Site](../../02_The_Site/01_Rationale_and_Importance.md) modules take over.
*   **Day 3: the rest inventory.** Formal kit, food, and body audit (the Medic's check-ins from the [Party Playbooks](02_Party_Composition_Playbooks.md), now seated and thorough: feet, joints, hydration, the elder's chest, the child's appetite). The journey's deferred costs surface on day three, when adrenaline stands down — plan for the party to be *weaker* on day three than day one, and schedule accordingly.
*   **Nightly: the routine itself is the security system.** Fixed dusk assembly, fixed watch or concealment drill, fixed dawn check. Routine is what turns a camp into a settlement — and it is also what makes anomalies (the sound that doesn't fit, the track that wasn't there) instantly visible to everyone.

## 7. Common Failure Modes

*   **Momentum commitment.** Walking straight into the site at hour zero because the journey's emotional arc demands it. The occupied-site discovery then happens at conversational range, from weakness. Prevention: the overwatch halt is written into the route plan as the *destination* — the site is one scout loop beyond it.
*   **Untested water on day one.** "The dossier said the spring was clean" — the dossier is months or years old. Giardia or worse in week one, with the party's medical margin at its thinnest. Prevention: boil everything, day one, no exceptions, including the water that looks perfect.
*   **Fortress-first building.** Calories poured into a thorn wall while water production and the latrine wait. The wall impresses no one; the dysentery is thorough. Prevention: the priority ladder, posted and spoken; defense in week one is concealment + noise + routine, all of which are nearly free.
*   **The day-three collapse, unplanned.** Adrenaline stands down, deferred injuries and exhaustion arrive together, and the schedule assumed day three would be productive. Prevention: schedule day three as the rest inventory — make the collapse an appointment.
*   **Contesting or crowding an occupied site.** The single worst arrival decision. Every option — fallback, parley from observation, long detour — beats proximity without invitation. Prevention: the fallback dossier chosen before departure, so withdrawal is a plan, not a defeat.

## 8. Vocabulary of the Foundation

*   **Day-Three Collapse:** The predictable post-adrenaline trough of deferred injuries and exhaustion, scheduled in advance as the rest inventory.
*   **Fatigue Override:** The standing rule that undone work stays undone at dusk; sleep is a security asset.
*   **Interim Perimeter:** The 72-hour security posture — concealment siting, noise ground, watch-or-concealment — whose jobs are audibility and forgettability, never fortification.
*   **Noise Ground:** A band of dry brush, branches, and gravel laid across likely approaches; a zero-cost acoustic alarm.
*   **Overwatch Halt:** The concealed, watered stop 500 m–1 km short of the objective where the party rests and the site is verified; the true destination of the journey plan.
*   **Priority Ladder:** The day-one work order set by the physiology of dying — water, shelter-for-the-vulnerable, perimeter, fire discipline, food last.
*   **Scout Loop:** The two-person perimeter-then-spiral reconnaissance that attempts to falsify the site dossier before the party commits.
