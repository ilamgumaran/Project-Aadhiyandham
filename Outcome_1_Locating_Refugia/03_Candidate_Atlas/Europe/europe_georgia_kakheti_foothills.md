# Candidate Location Evaluation: Caucasus Foothills, Kakheti (Europe)

**Alignment:** Outcome 1: Locating and Connecting Optimal Refugia

## 1. Location Overview
*   **Name:** Kakheti Foothills (Alazani Valley)
*   **Coordinates/Region:** Eastern Georgia (Caucasus)
*   **GPS Coordinates:** 41.7500° N, 45.7167° E
*   **Base Elevation:** ~250m - 500m
*   **Primary Biome:** Temperate Deciduous Forest / Steppe

## 2. Phase I: Placement & Arrival (Pass/Fail Criteria)
*   [x] **Elevation:** 100m+ above sea level? Yes.
*   [x] **Protection:** Shielded by a major mountain range? Yes. The Greater Caucasus Mountains act as a massive thermal wall.
*   [x] **Gravity Water:** Year-round water sources from higher elevation? Yes. Fed by the Alazani and Iori rivers and high-altitude springs.
*   **Notes:** Historically stable climate; the "cradle of wine" with 8,000 years of continuous agricultural heritage.

## 3. Phase II: Stabilization & Sustenance
*   **Solar Potential:** High. Intense solar radiation on south-facing slopes; clear mountain air.
*   **Soil Type:** **Cinnamonic soils** (iron-rich clays) and black slate. Mineral-dense and naturally fertile.
*   **Biomass Availability:** High. Extensive forests of oak, beech, and chestnut in the surrounding foothills.
*   **Agricultural Score (9.5/10):** Exceptional for grains, vines, nuts, and stone fruits. The "Qvevri" heritage provides a template for zero-energy food preservation.

## 4. Phase III: The Human System
*   **Navigability:** The Alazani valley floor is a broad, flat plain ideal for animal-powered transport.
*   **Climate & Mood:** Stable and ventilated. The majestic Caucasus peaks provide a profound psychological sense of security.
*   **Health Score (9/10):** Elite mineral water and air. Ancient cultural focus on fermented foods (gut health).

## 5. Phase IV & V: Wisdom & Synthesis
*   **Geological Foundations:** Abundant slate, limestone, and clays. Ancient history of stone architecture and qvevri-making.
*   **Isolation vs. Network:** A natural fortress with high internal capacity. Strategically located between the ruins of the Black Sea and Caspian basins.

## 6. Final Verdict
*   **Recommendation:** **PROCEED (Top Tier Ancient Stability Candidate)**
*   **Justification:** Kakheti is a "proven" civilization cradle. Its combination of mountain protection, fertile soil, and ancient preservation wisdom makes it an elite candidate for re-establishing life.

## 7. Practical Implementation Guide for Beginners

### 7.1. Step-by-Step Action Plan
*   **Preparation:** Read this module fully before attempting to build or implement. Gather raw materials locally.
*   **Execution:** Build a small-scale prototype first. For example, if building a mechanical system or shelter, make a miniature version to test physics and material strength.
*   **Review:** Test the implementation under stress (wind, water, heavy use) and refine.

### 7.2. Troubleshooting & Failure Modes
*   **System Failure:** When a system breaks, apply the "5 Whys" technique to find the root cause (e.g., The gear broke. Why? It was made of soft wood. Why? We lacked hardwood. Why?). Fix the root cause, not just the symptom.
*   **Burnout:** Decadal survival is a marathon, not a sprint. If the community is exhausted, lower productivity expectations and focus on rest and social cohesion.

### 7.3. Community & Decadal Flourishing
*   **Apprenticeship:** No critical skill should be held by only one person. Every master must have an apprentice. Document local modifications to these original instructions in a physical journal.
