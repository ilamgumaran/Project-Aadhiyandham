# Livestock and Mechanical Animals: Management Guide

## 1. Why This Is Important
In Aadhiyandham, animals are not merely food; they are biological tools. They provide the mechanical energy for plowing and transport, and the biochemical energy (manure) for the Terra Preta system. By integrating animals into the landscape (Silvopasture), we create a self-sustaining loop where the animals protect the trees, and the trees provide feed and shelter for the animals.

## 2. Expected Outcome
A closed-loop nutrient system where animal labor replaces petroleum-based machinery, and animal waste eliminates the need for synthetic fertilizer.

---

## 3. How-To: Selecting and Training Draft Animals

### The Ox (The Primary Engine)
*   **Selection:** Use steers (castrated males) of hardy local breeds.
*   **Training:** Start training at 6 months. Focus on "Voice Commands" (Gee = Right, Haw = Left, Whoa = Stop). 
*   **Equipment:** Use a wooden **Neck Yoke**. It is simple to carve from local hardwood (Module 03) and distributes the weight across the animal's powerful shoulders.

### The Mule (The Logistical Engine)
*   **Selection:** Cross of a male donkey and female horse.
*   **Benefit:** Mules have "harder" hooves than horses, meaning they require fewer shoes, and they have a natural sense of self-preservation that prevents them from being overworked.

---

## 4. How-To: Rotational Grazing (The Silvopasture Loop)
Do not let animals stay in one field for more than 3-5 days.

### Step 1: The Perimeter
Use "Living Fences" (hedges of thorny or dense trees like Osage Orange or Hawthorn) to contain large animals.

### Step 2: The Rotation
1.  **Phase 1 (The Meadow):** Animals graze the high grass.
2.  **Phase 2 (The Orchard):** Move animals to the orchard or forest benches to eat fallen fruit and clear the understory.
3.  **Phase 3 (The Recovery):** Move animals to a new zone. The previous zone now has a layer of manure and needs 30 days to regrow.

### Step 3: Manure Harvesting
**CRITICAL:** Daily collection of fresh manure is required for the **Terra Preta activation** (Module 02). Store manure in a covered pit to prevent nitrogen loss through rainwater leaching.

## 6. Maintenance Guide
*   **Veterinary Care:** Focus on preventative health (Module 04). Clean water and rotation are the best defense against parasites.
*   **Hoof Care:** Learn basic farriery (Module 03) to trim hooves and manage foot health in humid mountain environments.
