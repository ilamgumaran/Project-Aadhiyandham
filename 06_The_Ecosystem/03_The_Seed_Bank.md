# The Seed Bank: Planting and Saving Guide

## 1. Why This Is Important
In Aadhiyandham, we exclusively use **Open-Pollinated Heirloom Seeds**. By saving seeds every year, you adapt your crops to your specific microclimate.

## 2. Expected Outcome
A permanent, locally-adapted source of high-density calories that requires zero external inputs.

---

## 3. How-To: Build a Winnowing Basket (The Seed Cleaner)
For grains like Quinoa and Buckwheat, you must separate the seed from the chaff (hulls).

### Materials Needed
*   **Flexible Rods:** Thin willow, hazel, or split bamboo.
*   **Binder:** Natural fiber twine or split rattan.

### Instructions
1.  Weave a shallow, wide-mouthed tray (approx. 50cm diameter).
2.  Ensure the weave is tight in the center but slightly more open near the edges.
3.  **How to Use:** Place dried seed heads in the basket. Toss the seeds into the air during a light breeze. The heavy seeds fall back into the basket, while the light chaff is blown away.

---

## 4. How-To: Managing the Primary Staples
*   **Potatoes:** Hill plants to encourage tubers; store in a root cellar.
*   **Corn:** Plant in blocks for wind pollination; save kernels from dry husks.
*   **Quinoa:** Winnow using the basket above once seeds feel like flint.

---

## 5. How-To: The Art of Seed Saving
1.  **Isolation:** Plant varieties 500m apart to prevent cross-pollination.
2.  **Selection:** Save seeds only from the strongest 10% of plants.
3.  **Storage:** Use airtight glass jars in the coolest part of the shelter.

## 🚀 Practical Implementation Guide for Beginners: The Ecosystem

### 1. Step-by-Step Action Plan
*   **Month 1:** Start a compost pile immediately. Collect all organic waste (excluding meat/dairy initially) and layer with dry carbon (leaves, straw).
*   **Month 3:** Begin creating Terra Preta (biochar). Dig a trench, burn wood with restricted oxygen, crush the char, and mix it with compost before adding to garden beds.
*   **Year 1:** Plant the 'Top 10 Staples' (Potatoes, Beans, Corn, Squash) from the seed bank. Save 20% of the yield strictly for next year's seeds.

### 2. Troubleshooting & Failure Modes
*   **Crop Failure (Pests):** Do not use chemical pesticides. Introduce companion planting (e.g., Marigolds with Tomatoes) and encourage natural predators like ladybugs and birds.
*   **Soil Depletion:** If yields drop, you are taking more than you give. Rest the bed, plant nitrogen-fixing cover crops (clover, beans), and double the compost application.

### 3. Community & Decadal Flourishing
*   Assign a "Keeper of the Seeds." Seed genetics adapt to your specific microclimate over decades. This is the true wealth of the settlement.
