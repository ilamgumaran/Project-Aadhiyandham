# 03 Mechanics, Infrastructure & Synthesis

This module focuses on building the hardware of civilization using mechanical advantage and material science, emphasizing repairability and local synthesis.

## 1. Mechanical Power & The Microgrid
Energy must be generated and applied with minimal high-tech dependencies.

### 1.1. Mechanical Advantage (Line-Shaft Systems)
To power a centralized workshop from a single prime mover (water wheel or windmill), a **Line-Shaft** system is the standard.
*   **Main Shaft:** 1.5–2 inch cold-rolled steel, supported by hangers every 8–10 feet.
*   **Pulley Ratios:** Speed is managed by the formula $N_1 \times D_1 = N_2 \times D_2$.
*   **Fast and Loose Pulleys:** A safety system where a belt can be shifted between a "fast" pulley (keyed to the shaft) and a "loose" pulley (spinning freely) to engage or disengage individual machines without stopping the main drive.
*   **Belt Tracking:** Pulleys must be "crowned" (thicker in the center) to keep flat belts centered.

## 2. Low-Tech Mobility & Infrastructure
Transportation systems designed for durability and multi-modal use.

### 2.1. Hybrid Bamboo/Steel Cargo Trike
A high-capacity (up to 250kg) vehicle using local and salvaged materials.
*   **Chassis:** Twin-boom design using thick-walled structural bamboo (e.g., Guadua or Tonkin).
*   **Steel Interfaces:** Salvage the **Head Tube**, **Bottom Bracket**, and **Dropouts** from scrap steel frames. These must be steel to handle the high precision and mechanical stress of bearings and threading.
*   **Joints (The Lug Method):** Miter the bamboo to the steel parts and wrap the joints with epoxy-soaked natural fibers (hemp, flax, or manila rope). This creates a composite "lug" stronger than the bamboo itself.

## 3. Material Synthesis & Recycling (The Bloomery)
Generating primary materials from the earth.

### 3.1. Small-Scale Iron Smelting (The Bloomery Furnace)
A shaft furnace capable of producing wrought iron from raw ore at approx. $1,200^\circ\text{C}$.
*   **Construction:** A clay/sand chimney (25cm internal diameter, 1m height) with walls 20cm thick for insulation.
*   **Process:** 
    1.  **Roast & Crush:** Heat iron ore in a wood fire to make it brittle, then crush to pea-sized.
    2.  **Charge:** Alternate layers of hardwood charcoal and ore in a 1:1 weight ratio.
    3.  **The Bloom:** After 5–8 hours, break the furnace base to extract the "bloom"—a spongy mass of iron and slag.
    4.  **Shingling:** Strike the hot bloom on an anvil to squeeze out liquid slag and weld the iron into a solid bar.

## 4. Workspaces & Tooling
*   **Standardization:** Use the SI system (Metric) for all new fabrication to ensure future compatibility and interchangeability of parts.
