# Material Synthesis: The Bloomery Iron Guide

## 1. Why This Is Important
The "Recycling Cliff" is real. Scavenged steel from the old world is a finite resource that will eventually corrode beyond use. To survive multi-generationally, a settlement must be able to synthesize iron directly from the earth. The **Bloomery Furnace** is the "mother tool" of civilization—the only technology that can turn rocks (ore) into the iron needed to make all other tools.

## 2. Expected Outcome
The production of a 2-5kg "Bloom" of high-quality wrought iron from raw local ore, capable of being forged into axes, chisels, and mechanical parts.

## 3. Materials Needed & Sourcing

| Material | Purpose | How to Find / Source |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Iron Ore** | The Source | Look for "Bog Iron" in wetlands (orange sludge) or "Magnetite" sands in riverbeds. |
| **Hardwood Charcoal**| The Fuel & Reducer | Produce in a pit kiln (Module 02) using dense woods (oak, hickory). |
| **Refractory Clay** | The Furnace | Dig below the topsoil. Mix with 50% sand to prevent cracking under high heat. |
| **Bellows** | The Oxygen | Build from animal hide and wood. Must provide a continuous blast of air. |

## 4. Assembly and Operation Instructions (Step-by-Step)

### Step 1: Building the Furnace (The Shaft)
1.  **Dimensions:** Build a clay chimney 1 meter tall with an internal diameter of 25cm.
2.  **The Tuyere:** Insert a ceramic or metal pipe (the tuyere) into the base of the furnace, angled slightly downward. This is where the air enters.
3.  **The Arch:** Leave a temporary opening at the very bottom (the tap-hole) to remove slag and eventually the iron bloom.

### Step 2: Preparing the Ore
**CRITICAL:** Raw ore must be "Roasted."
1.  Build a large wood fire and place the ore rocks on top.
2.  Heat until glowing red, then quench in water.
3.  **Result:** The ore becomes brittle. Crush it into pea-sized pieces.

### Step 3: The Smelt (The Burn)
1.  **Pre-heat:** Fill the furnace with wood and charcoal and burn for 2 hours to dry the clay.
2.  **Charging:** Once hot, add a layer of charcoal, then a layer of crushed ore (1:1 weight ratio).
3.  **Blast:** Pump the bellows continuously. The temperature must reach approx. 1200°C.
4.  **Slag Tapping:** Every hour, use a rod to clear the tap-hole. Molten glass-like "slag" will flow out. This is a good sign.

### Step 4: Extracting the Bloom
1.  After 6-8 hours of charging, stop adding ore and burn down the remaining charcoal.
2.  Break open the front arch of the furnace.
3.  Using heavy tongs, pull out the white-hot "Bloom"—a spongy mass of iron and trapped slag.

## 6. Maintenance Guide
*   **The First Forge:** Immediately take the hot bloom to an anvil and strike it with a heavy hammer (Shingling). This "welds" the iron together and squeezes out the remaining slag.
*   **Furnace Reuse:** A well-built clay furnace can be repaired with fresh daub and used for 10-20 smelts before needing a full rebuild.
