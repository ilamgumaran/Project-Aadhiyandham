## 1. Foundational Knowledge of the Human Body
### 1.1. The Brain's Biochemical Signals (The Rhythms of Life)
Understanding the endocrine responses to environment is critical for maintaining a "centered null" state.
*   **The Sunlight Signal (SCN):** Sunlight enters the eyes and stimulates the Suprachiasmatic Nucleus, triggering **Serotonin** (mood, focus, calm) and suppressing **Melatonin**.
*   **The Sleep Drive:** Throughout the day, **Adenosine** builds up, creating sleep pressure. As light fades, the pineal gland releases **Melatonin**, lowering core temperature and blood pressure.
*   **The Wake-Up Call:** In the early morning, a spike of **Cortisol** increases alertness and blood sugar.
*   **The Cleaning Cycle (Glymphatic System):** During deep sleep, the brain activates a "wash" cycle, using cerebrospinal fluid to flush out metabolic waste and toxins.

## 2. Medicine & Apothecary
### 2.1. The Temperate Antibiotic Kit
Five foundational plants for infection control and antiseptic use:
1.  **Garlic (*Allium sativum*):** Active compound: **Allicin**. Broad-spectrum antibacterial and antifungal. Best used raw/crushed for respiratory and digestive infections.
2.  **Thyme (*Thymus vulgaris*):** Active compound: **Thymol**. Strong antiseptic and expectorant. Excellent for sore throats (tea/gargle) and skin disinfectants.
3.  **Echinacea:** Native temperate grassland plant; acts as an immunostimulant to fight early-stage viral and bacterial infections.
4.  **Sage (*Salvia officinalis*):** Rich in tannins and volatile oils. Antiseptic for mouth and throat infections (gingivitis, ulcers).
5.  **Plantain (*Plantago lanceolata*):** Common "field medicine." Crushed leaves provide **Aucubin** for treating stings, bites, and minor wounds to prevent infection.

## 3. How the World Works (The Archive of Reason)
### 3.1. Physics: The 6 Simple Machines
Mechanical Advantage (MA) = Output Force / Input Force.
*   **The Lever:** $MA = \text{Dist. to Input} / \text{Dist. to Output}$ (from fulcrum).
*   **The Inclined Plane:** $MA = \text{Length of Slope} / \text{Height}$.
*   **The Wheel and Axle:** $MA = \text{Radius of Wheel} / \text{Radius of Axle}$.
*   **The Pulley:** $MA = \text{Number of rope segments supporting the load}$.
*   **The Wedge:** $MA = \text{Length of Wedge} / \text{Width}$.
*   **The Screw:** $MA = \text{Circumference} / \text{Pitch (thread distance)}$.
