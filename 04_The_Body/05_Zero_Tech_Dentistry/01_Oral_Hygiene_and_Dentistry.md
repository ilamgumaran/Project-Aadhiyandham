## 2. The Mechanics of Zero-Tech Oral Hygiene

### The Root Cause: Diet over Brushing
Tooth decay is not caused by a lack of fluoride; it is caused by the fermentation of refined carbohydrates (sugars and white flour) by *Streptococcus mutans* bacteria in the mouth, which produces enamel-dissolving acid.
*   **The Aadhiyandham Diet:** A settlement surviving on complex carbohydrates (potatoes, whole beans), meat, and wild greens will experience a 90% reduction in dental caries compared to a modern diet. 

### The Botanical Toothbrush (The Miswak / Neem Stick)
You do not need plastic bristles or synthetic paste.
*   **The Tool:** Cut a small twig (approx. 1/4 inch thick) from a specific tree—*Salvadora persica* (Miswak), Neem, or White Oak. 
*   **The Preparation:** Chew the end of the twig until the bark comes off and the inner wood fibers separate into a stiff, bristly brush.
*   **The Chemistry:** These specific woods contain naturally occurring silica (which acts as a mild abrasive to remove plaque), tannins (which act as astringents to tighten gums), and potent antimicrobial resins. 

### Saltwater Rinsing
*   **Osmotic Flush:** Vigorous rinsing with warm saltwater (1 teaspoon of salt per cup of water) forces fluid through the gum tissue via osmosis, reducing inflammation and creating an alkaline environment where acid-producing bacteria cannot thrive.

## 3. Emergency Extraction Protocols
If a tooth becomes necrotic and begins to swell the jaw, it must be removed to save the patient's life.
*   **Anesthesia:** Boil the inner bark of the Willow tree (which contains salicin, the chemical precursor to aspirin) to manage pain, though it will not numb the localized area like modern novocaine. Clove oil applied directly to the exposed nerve acts as a powerful topical analgesic.
*   **Elevation over Pulling:** A tooth should not be simply "yanked." A sterile, flat metal tool (an elevator) is wedged between the tooth and the bone to slowly rock the tooth back and forth. This severs the periodontal ligaments holding it in place until the tooth is loose enough to be removed with minimal trauma to the jawbone.
