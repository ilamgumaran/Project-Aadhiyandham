# Candidate Location Evaluation: Okanagan Valley (Lower), BC (Canada)

## Location Overview
*   **Name:** Lower Okanagan Valley (Osoyoos / Oliver)
*   **Coordinates/Region:** Southern British Columbia, Canada
*   **Base Elevation:** ~280m - 400m
*   **Primary Biome:** Semi-Arid Shrub-Steppe / Ponderosa Pine Forest

## Phase I: Placement & Arrival (Pass/Fail Criteria)
*   [x] **Elevation:** 100m+ above sea level? Yes (~300m).
*   [x] **Protection:** Shielded by a major mountain range? Yes. Nestled in the rain shadow of the Coast and Cascade mountains.
*   [x] **Gravity Water:** Year-round water sources from higher elevation? Yes. Fed by snowmelt from the Monashee mountains.
*   **Notes:** The "Thermal Belt" of Canada; one of the only semi-arid refuges in the northern latitudes.

## Phase II: Stabilization & Sustenance
*   **Solar Potential:** Very High. The sunniest region in BC; ideal for solar energy and passive heating.
*   **Soil Type:** Silt and glacial till benches. Fertile when irrigated; requires organic matter to buffer against summer drought.
*   **Biomass Availability:** Moderate. Ponderosa pine and Douglas fir; grasslands provide high-value carbon sinks.
*   **Agricultural Score (8.5/10):** Elite for stone fruits, grapes, and heat-tolerant heirloom vegetables. Irrigation is the primary requirement.

## Phase III: The Human System
*   **Navigability:** Excellent. Flat lake benches are ideal for non-electric transport.
*   **Climate & Mood:** Dry and luminous. The "desert-like" clarity and open views support a focused, alert mental state.
*   **Health Score (8.5/10):** Low humidity and clean air. Historically a health destination for respiratory recovery.

## Phase IV & V: Wisdom & Synthesis
*   **Geological Foundations:** Abundant stone, sand, and river gravels.
*   **Isolation vs. Network:** Naturally defensible via narrow valley passes. Close enough to the ruins of Vancouver/Kelowna for salvage potential.

## Final Verdict
*   **Recommendation:** **PROCEED (Top Tier Northern Refugia)**
*   **Justification:** The Lower Okanagan offers a rare "warm-dry" northern candidate. For a society prioritizing solar energy and high-value horticulture in a northern latitude, this is the premier site.
