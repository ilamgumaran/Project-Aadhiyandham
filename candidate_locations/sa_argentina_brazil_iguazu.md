# Candidate Location Evaluation: Iguazu National Park, Argentina/Brazil

## Location Overview
*   **Name:** Iguazu National Park (Basaltic Plateau)
*   **Coordinates/Region:** Misiones, Argentina / Paraná, Brazil
*   **GPS Coordinates:** 25.6867° S, 54.4447° W
*   **Base Elevation:** ~150m - 250m
*   **Primary Biome:** Subtropical Rainforest

## Phase I: Placement & Arrival (Pass/Fail Criteria)
*   [x] **Elevation:** 100m+ above sea level? Yes.
*   [x] **Protection:** Shielded by topographic complexity? Yes. The basaltic plateau and canyon walls provide thermal buffering.
*   [x] **Gravity Water:** Year-round water sources from higher elevation? Yes. Massive water volume from the Iguazu Falls and upper river systems.
*   **Notes:** High humidity and constant "mist zone" provide extreme ecological stability.

## Phase II: Stabilization & Sustenance
*   **Solar Potential:** High. Consistent subtropical sunshine.
*   **Soil Type:** Rich, volcanic basalt-derived soils. Extremely fertile.
*   **Biomass Availability:** Extreme. Intact primary rainforest provides endless resources.
*   **Agricultural Score (9/10):** Elite for tropical and subtropical staples.

## Phase III: The Human System
*   **Navigability:** Plateau areas are flat and navigable; river canyons are steep and defensible.
*   **Climate & Mood:** Warm and humid. The constant sound of falling water provides a powerful acoustic anchor.
*   **Health Score (8/10):** High mineral water security.

## Phase IV & V: Wisdom & Synthesis
*   **Geological Foundations:** Exceptional basalt for building. Proximity to historical trade routes.
*   **Isolation vs. Network:** Naturally defensible via the falls and dense forest.

## Final Verdict
*   **Recommendation:** **PROCEED (Top Tier Tropical Candidate)**
*   **Justification:** Combines elite volcanic fertility with unmatched water security and extreme biomass growth rates.
