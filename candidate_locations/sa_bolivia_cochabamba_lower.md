# Candidate Location Evaluation: Cochabamba Lower Reaches, Bolivia (South America)

## Location Overview
*   **Name:** Cochabamba Valley (Lower Terraces)
*   **Coordinates/Region:** Cochabamba Department, Bolivia
*   **Base Elevation:** ~2,500m (Basin Floor) - *Note: High-altitude exception.*
*   **Primary Biome:** Subtropical Highland (Mesothermal)

## Phase I: Placement & Arrival (Pass/Fail Criteria)
*   [x] **Elevation:** 100m+ above sea level? Yes (Extreme altitude).
*   [x] **Protection:** Shielded by a major mountain range? Yes. Nestled in the Cordillera Oriental of the Andes.
*   [x] **Gravity Water:** Year-round water sources from higher elevation? Yes. Fed by snowmelt and mountain runoff.
*   **Notes:** The "City of Eternal Spring." Stable year-round temperatures (22°C–26°C) due to inter-mountain positioning.

## Phase II: Stabilization & Sustenance
*   **Solar Potential:** Extreme. High-altitude tropical sun; exceptionally high direct solar yield.
*   **Soil Type:** Rich alluvial and lacustrine soils (ancient lake bed). Historically Bolivia's "granary."
*   **Biomass Availability:** Moderate. Mountain scrub and forests; requires careful management of water to sustain high biomass.
*   **Agricultural Score (8.5/10):** Exceptional for maize, wheat, barley, and various stone fruits. Water scarcity is the primary limiting factor, necessitating "Water War" level communal management.

## Phase III: The Human System
*   **Navigability:** The basin floor is relatively flat and easily navigated.
*   **Climate & Mood:** Exceptionally stable and mild. The clear, intense light supports a highly alert and curious mental state.
*   **Health Score (8/10):** Pure mountain air and water (at the source). High altitude requires physiological adaptation.

## Phase IV & V: Wisdom & Synthesis
*   **Geological Foundations:** Abundant sandstone, river rock, and clays. Proximity to major Andean mineral deposits (tin, silver).
*   **Isolation vs. Network:** Naturally defensible inter-mountain basin. Historically a center of social and indigenous rights movements (The Water War).

## Final Verdict
*   **Recommendation:** **PROCEED (Top Tier High-Altitude Candidate)**
*   **Justification:** For a society looking for extreme climatic stability and solar energy, Cochabamba is elite. Its "Eternal Spring" climate reduces energy needs to almost zero, provided water management is communally secured.
