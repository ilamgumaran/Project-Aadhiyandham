# Candidate Location Evaluation: Sichuan Basin Benches, China (Asia)

## Location Overview
*   **Name:** Sichuan Basin (Peripheral Hills/Benches)
*   **Coordinates/Region:** Southwest China (Sichuan Province)
*   **Base Elevation:** ~250m - 500m
*   **Primary Biome:** Humid Subtropical Forest

## Phase I: Placement & Arrival (Pass/Fail Criteria)
*   [x] **Elevation:** 100m+ above sea level? Yes (~250m base).
*   [x] **Protection:** Shielded by a major mountain range? Yes. Completely encircled by mountains, most notably the Qinling range to the north which blocks Siberian air.
*   [x] **Gravity Water:** Year-round water sources from higher elevation? Yes. Massive river systems (Yangtze tributaries) flow from the Tibetan plateau.
*   **Notes:** Known as the "Purple Basin" due to its unique, stable, and humid microclimate. Often foggy, providing natural concealment and UV protection.

## Phase II: Stabilization & Sustenance
*   **Solar Potential:** Moderate. High cloud/fog cover reduces direct solar, but the diffuse light is excellent for specific crops.
*   **Soil Type:** **Purple Soil** (weathered red sandstone/shale). Extremely rich in phosphorus, potassium, and calcium. One of the most fertile soils on Earth.
*   **Biomass Availability:** High. Dense sub-tropical vegetation and extensive bamboo forests.
*   **Agricultural Score (10/10):** The "Land of Abundance." Can support multiple harvests per year. Ideal for rice, wheat, citrus, and a vast array of medicinal herbs.

## Phase III: The Human System
*   **Navigability:** Flat-topped hills and basins are easily navigated. Ancient irrigation systems (Dujiangyan) demonstrate the ease of water movement.
*   **Climate & Mood:** Humid and mild. The "pocket" effect creates a sense of enclosure and security, though the lack of direct sun can impact mood (Module 04).
*   **Health Score (8/10):** Deep heritage of Traditional Chinese Medicine (TCM) based on local flora. High humidity requires careful management of respiratory health.

## Phase IV & V: Wisdom & Synthesis
*   **Geological Foundations:** Rich in shales, clays, and salt (Zigong region). Abundant iron ore historically mined in the periphery.
*   **Isolation vs. Network:** A natural fortress. The "Road to Shu" was historically considered impassable to invaders. High internal carrying capacity allows for a self-sufficient civilization.

## Final Verdict
*   **Recommendation:** **PROCEED (Top Tier Carrying Capacity)**
*   **Justification:** The Sichuan Basin is perhaps the most resilient agricultural "engine" in Asia. Its mountain protection and elite soil fertility make it a primary candidate for a large-scale sustainable civilization.
