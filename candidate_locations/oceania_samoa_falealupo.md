# Candidate Location Evaluation: Falealupo Peninsula, Samoa

## Location Overview
*   **Name:** Falealupo Peninsula (Coastal Rainforest)
*   **Coordinates/Region:** Savai'i Island, Samoa
*   **GPS Coordinates:** 13.5025° S, 172.7897° W
*   **Base Elevation:** ~100m - 300m (Low-altitude volcanic ridges)
*   **Primary Biome:** Lowland Tropical Rainforest

## Phase I: Placement & Arrival (Pass/Fail Criteria)
*   [x] **Elevation:** 100m+ above sea level? Yes.
*   [x] **Protection:** Shielded by complex volcanic terrain? Yes. The rugged interior provides microhabitats resilient to cyclone damage.
*   [x] **Gravity Water:** Year-round water sources from higher elevation? Yes. Fed by volcanic springs and consistent rainfall.
*   **Notes:** One of the last large tracts of lowland tropical rainforest in the Pacific.

## Phase II: Stabilization & Sustenance
*   **Solar Potential:** High. Consistent tropical solar gain.
*   **Soil Type:** Fertile volcanic (Basalt-derived) soils. Extremely productive.
*   **Biomass Availability:** Extreme. Dense rainforest and high growth rates.
*   **Agricultural Score (9/10):** Exceptional for tropical staples (Taro, Breadfruit, Coconuts).

## Phase III: The Human System
*   **Navigability:** Lower ridges are navigable; forest requires careful clearing.
*   **Climate & Mood:** Warm and stable. The "covenant" culture provides a template for communal protection.
*   **Health Score (8/10):** Exceptional air and water quality.

## Phase IV & V: Wisdom & Synthesis
*   **Geological Foundations:** Abundant volcanic basalt for building. Ancient history of Samoan resource management.
*   **Isolation vs. Network:** Naturally isolated on the western tip of Savai'i. Highly defensible via distance and terrain.

## Final Verdict
*   **Recommendation:** **PROCEED (Top Tier Pacific Candidate)**
*   **Justification:** The ultimate tropical island refugia. Combines volcanic fertility with extreme biomass and a stable, community-protected environment.
