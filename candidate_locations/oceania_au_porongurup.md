# Candidate Location Evaluation: Porongurup National Park, Australia

## Location Overview
*   **Name:** Porongurup National Park (Lower Slopes)
*   **Coordinates/Region:** Great Southern Region, Western Australia
*   **GPS Coordinates:** 34.6743° S, 117.8705° E
*   **Base Elevation:** ~200m - 300m
*   **Primary Biome:** Karri and Jarrah Forest

## Phase I: Placement & Arrival (Pass/Fail Criteria)
*   [x] **Elevation:** 100m+ above sea level? Yes.
*   [x] **Protection:** Shielded by major mountain range? Yes. The ancient granite domes of the Porongurups act as a microclimate buffer.
*   [x] **Gravity Water:** Year-round water sources from higher elevation? Yes. Numerous springs and streams flow from the granite peaks.
*   **Notes:** A unique "island" of high-rainfall forest surrounded by flatter, drier agricultural land.

## Phase II: Stabilization & Sustenance
*   **Solar Potential:** High. Stable Mediterranean sunlight.
*   **Soil Type:** Fertile granitic loams. Excellent for diverse horticulture.
*   **Biomass Availability:** High. Dense native forest and proximity to established plantations.
*   **Agricultural Score (8/10):** Exceptional for cool-temperate fruits and vegetables. Granite-derived soils are mineral-rich.

## Phase III: The Human System
*   **Navigability:** Lower slopes are navigable; steeper sections provide natural protection.
*   **Climate & Mood:** Temperate and stable. The ancient "deep time" presence of the granite domes supports a centered mindset.
*   **Health Score (9/10):** Elite air and water quality.

## Phase IV & V: Wisdom & Synthesis
*   **Geological Foundations:** Abundant granite for building. High mineral diversity.
*   **Isolation vs. Network:** Isolated Great Southern location; defensible via the range itself.

## Final Verdict
*   **Recommendation:** **PROCEED (Top Tier Secondary Candidate)**
*   **Justification:** A rare "biological island" providing extreme stability and high-quality water in a stable maritime-influenced region.
