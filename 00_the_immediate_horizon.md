# 00 The Immediate Horizon: The First 100 Days

This module is the "Quick Start" guide. It covers the critical period of transition from arrival to the establishment of foundational systems.

## 1. Immediate Bio-Security
### 1.1. Water Purification (The 50-Person Filter)
To support a pioneer population of 50, a **Slow Sand Filter (SSF)** with a Biochar stage is the primary objective.
*   **Capacity:** 2,000 Liters/day (40L per person).
*   **Design:** A vertical tank (1.05m diameter, 2.5m height).
*   **Layers (Top to Bottom):**
    1.  **Water Head (1.0m):** Provides gravity pressure.
    2.  **Bio-Layer (Schmutzdecke):** Biological film that consumes pathogens (requires 10-14 days to ripen).
    3.  **Fine Sand (0.8m):** 0.15–0.35mm washed masonry sand.
    4.  **Biochar/Charcoal (0.2m):** Adsorbs chemicals and odors.
    5.  **Graded Gravel (0.3m):** Supports the bed and prevents clogging.
*   **Crucial Rule:** The filter must run 24/7 to keep the bio-layer alive. The outlet must rise above the sand level to ensure the bed never runs dry.

## 2. Shelter & Thermal Grounding
### 2.1. Immediate Construction Material Comparison
*   **Wattle and Daub (Insulative Focus):** 
    *   **Pros:** Faster to build; higher R-value (R-1 to R-3) due to straw/fiber content and air pockets.
    *   **Best Use:** Sleeping quarters and initial workshops where warmth retention is a priority.
*   **Rammed Earth (Thermal Mass Focus):**
    *   **Pros:** Massive thermal inertia ("flywheel effect"); absorbs day heat and releases it at night.
    *   **Best Use:** High-occupancy common areas or food storage where temperature stability is more important than raw insulation.
    *   **Design Note:** Walls must be 18+ inches thick to achieve a 10-12 hour thermal lag.

## 3. Emergency Nutrition (The Mountain Scavenge)
In the first 100 days, before the first harvest, utilize these 10 universal emergency sources:
1.  **Pine Nuts:** High fat/protein; scavenge from mature cones or squirrel caches.
2.  **Inner Bark (Cambium):** Pine/Birch/Spruce; starch-rich; scrape and boil.
3.  **Cattail Roots:** Pure starch; dig rhizomes from marsh edges and roast or crush for flour.
4.  **Dandelion Roots:** Complex carbs; boil to reduce bitterness.
5.  **Pine Needle Tea:** Essential Vitamin C to prevent scurvy and fatigue.
6.  **Wild Berries:** Simple sugars for immediate energy (stick to aggregate berries like raspberries).
7.  **Grass Seeds:** Carbohydrates; rub seed heads and boil into a gruel.
8.  **Rock Tripe (Lichen):** Carbs; boil extensively to remove bitter acids.
9.  **Earthworms/Grubs:** High protein; must be cooked to kill parasites.
10. **Rose Hips:** Massive Vitamin C; eat outer flesh, avoid hairy internal seeds.
