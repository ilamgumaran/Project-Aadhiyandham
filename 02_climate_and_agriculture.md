# 02 Climate, Soil & Seed

In a post-industrial world, agriculture must be regenerative, utilizing closed-loop systems that require zero synthetic inputs.

## 1. Climate Resilience & Projections
The land must be able to support life across a 100-year horizon of climatic volatility.

### 1.1. Agricultural Zones (A-Z)
*   **Growing Season Minima:** A minimum of 120 frost-free days per year for staple calorie crops.
*   **Heat Tolerance:** Selection of varieties that can survive spike events (>40°C) without total crop failure.

## 2. Soil Sovereignty (The Terra Preta Method)
Building a "living" soil is the primary objective. The Amazonian Dark Earth (Terra Preta) model is our standard for carbon-sequestering, high-fertility soil.

### 2.1. The Activation Ratio (Charging the Biochar)
Raw biochar must be "charged" to avoid nitrogen theft from the soil.
*   **Optimal Ratio:** **1 Part Biochar to 1 Part Compost/Manure (50:50 mix).**
*   **Activation Period:** Mix and keep moist for 2–4 weeks before use. Add crushed eggshells (calcium) and fish bones for a complete mineral profile.

### 2.2. Application
*   **Topsoil Integration:** Aim for **10% biochar by volume** in the top 4–6 inches of garden beds.
*   **High-Demand Areas:** Increase up to 20% for sandy soils to maximize water and nutrient retention.

## 3. The Seed Bank (The 10 Universal Staples)
Prioritizing heirloom (open-pollinated) varieties with the highest calorie and nutrient density per acre.
1.  **Taro:** (18–38M cal/acre) The highest calorie producer in wetland or tropical microclimates.
2.  **Potatoes (German Butterball):** (15–25M cal/acre) High Vitamin C and Potassium.
3.  **Cassava/Yuca:** (12–15M cal/acre) Drought-tolerant "living storage" carbohydrate.
4.  **Sweet Potatoes:** (10–15M cal/acre) Massive Vitamin A and antioxidants.
5.  **Sunchokes (Jerusalem Artichokes):** (10–12M cal/acre) Prolific, iron-rich, and maintenance-free.
6.  **Corn (Glass Gem/Flint):** (5–8M cal/acre) Superior for nutrient-dense flour production.
7.  **Quinoa:** (3–5M cal/acre) A complete protein with all essential amino acids.
8.  **Amaranth:** (3–5M cal/acre) High protein and iron; both seeds and leaves are edible.
9.  **Buckwheat:** (Fast 60-90 day crop) High magnesium; thrives in poor soil where others fail.
10. **Millet (Finger Millet):** High calcium and drought resistance; stores for years without spoilage.

## 4. Livestock & Mechanical Animals
Animals are the mechanical advantage and fertilizer engines of the settlement.

### 4.1. The Work-to-Feed Efficiency Scale
*   **Oxen (The Economy Engine):** Highest efficiency for heavy labor on low-quality forage (grass/roughage). Best for land clearing and heavy plowing.
*   **Mules (The Endurance Engine):** 1/3 more feed-efficient than horses. Higher heat tolerance and superior longevity. Best for long-distance hauling and sustained plowing.
*   **Draft Horses:** High speed/power but lowest feed efficiency (requires grain and high-quality hay). Use only for time-critical tasks.
