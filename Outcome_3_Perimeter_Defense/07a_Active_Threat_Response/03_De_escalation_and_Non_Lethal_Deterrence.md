**Alignment:** Outcome 3: Perimeter Sovereignty and Passive Defense

## 1. Why This Is Important

Most human contacts in a post-collapse or remote settlement context are not hostile. Travelers, refugees, lost individuals, and small trading parties will be far more common than organized raiders. Responding to every contact with maximum force creates enemies, destroys potential alliances, and psychologically damages the responders. This module establishes a communication-first protocol that distinguishes travelers from threats without exposing the community to unnecessary risk.

## 2. Expected Outcome

A trained contact team of 2-4 individuals who can engage unknown humans at the perimeter using a structured de-escalation protocol, assess intent, and either welcome, trade with, redirect, or deter the contact — all without lethal force as a first resort.

## 4. Assembly Instructions (Step-by-Step)

### 4.1. Step 1: Establish the Contact Point

Designate a single location — the **Contact Point** — where all interactions with outsiders occur. This should be:
- At least 100 meters from the main settlement.
- Visible from a watch post (overwatch).
- On a terrain bottleneck where the community holds the high ground.
- Stocked with trade goods and signaling equipment.

Never allow unknown contacts to approach the settlement itself.

### 4.2. Step 2: Define the Contact Team

The contact team consists of:
- **Speaker:** The verbally skilled individual who communicates. Only one person speaks.
- **Observer:** Watches the contact's body language, scans for hidden companions, and maintains overwatch communication.
- **Backup (2 people):** Positioned out of sight behind cover, 20-30 meters back. They do not engage unless the Speaker signals distress.

### 4.3. Step 3: The Five-Phase De-escalation Protocol

**Phase 1 — Announce (distance: 80+ meters)**
Shout a clear, loud greeting from cover. Use the local language and a universal open-hand gesture. State: "We see you. We are peaceful. Stop and identify yourself."

**Phase 2 — Assess (distance: 50-80 meters)**
Ask three questions:
1. How many are in your group?
2. Where are you traveling to?
3. Do you need water or medical aid?

Observe: Are they armed? Are they malnourished? Do their numbers match their claim?

**Phase 3 — Decide**

| Assessment | Action |
|:---|:---|
| Solo traveler, unarmed, in distress | Offer water and food at the Contact Point. Do not reveal settlement location. |
| Small group, unarmed, seeking trade | Proceed to trade protocol at the Contact Point. |
| Armed group, cooperative | Request weapons be left at a visible spot before approaching Contact Point. |
| Armed group, non-cooperative | Escalate to Phase 4. |
| Large group (10+) of any disposition | Do not engage directly. Signal watch post. Observe from concealment. |

**Phase 4 — Deter (non-lethal)**
If the contact refuses to comply or shows hostile intent:
1. **Acoustic Deterrence:** Sound the settlement horn (continuous blast). This signals that the community is alert and organized.
2. **Visual Deterrence:** Light smoke signals. Multiple smoke columns create the illusion of a larger, more distributed population.
3. **Light Deterrence:** At night, shine multiple torches or mirrors. The goal is to make the settlement appear large, awake, and not worth the cost of attacking.

**Phase 5 — Withdraw**
If deterrence fails, the contact team withdraws to the settlement via a pre-planned route that the outsiders cannot follow easily (dense brush, hidden trail). Activate emergency lockdown (Module 04).

### 4.4. Step 4: Post-Contact Debrief

After every contact (friendly or hostile), the contact team debriefs the community:
- Who was contacted.
- What they wanted.
- Which direction they came from and departed to.
- Any intelligence gathered (other settlements, threats, resource availability).

Log all contacts in the settlement's permanent record.

## 6. Vocabulary of the Foundation

* **Contact Point:** A designated, fortified location away from the main settlement where all outsider interactions occur.
* **De-escalation Protocol:** A structured, phased communication process designed to reduce tension and assess intent before any physical confrontation.
* **Acoustic Deterrence:** Use of loud, organized sound (horns, drums) to signal alertness and discourage approach.
* **Overwatch:** A concealed observer providing situational awareness and backup communication during a contact event.

## 7. Scientific Validation & Research Context

* **Conflict Resolution Theory:** The Thomas-Kilmann model demonstrates that collaborative or accommodating responses to conflict produce more durable outcomes than competitive (aggressive) responses, particularly when ongoing coexistence is likely.
* **Deception in Defense:** Military doctrine on "force multiplication through deception" (FM 90-2) confirms that making a force appear larger than it is can deter engagement without any actual combat capability.
* **Anthropological Precedent:** Many indigenous societies (e.g., Sentinelese, various Amazonian groups) maintain buffer zones and ritualized contact protocols that have successfully protected their communities for centuries without standing armies.

## 8. Practical Implementation Guide for Beginners

### 8.1. Step-by-Step Action Plan
* **Preparation:** Designate and stock the Contact Point. Assign the first contact team based on temperament: the Speaker should be calm, articulate, and non-threatening in demeanor.
* **Execution:** Run monthly contact drills where community members role-play as outsiders with different dispositions (friendly, confused, aggressive). Practice until Phase 1-3 transitions are smooth.
* **Review:** After any real-world contact, evaluate what worked and what did not. Update the protocol based on actual experience.

### 8.2. Troubleshooting & Failure Modes
* **Speaker Freezes Under Pressure:** If the designated Speaker cannot perform in a real contact, the Observer must be cross-trained to assume the role immediately. Never have a single point of failure on the contact team.
* **Outsiders Bypass the Contact Point:** If contacts are approaching from unexpected directions, your passive perimeter (Section 07) has gaps. Fix the perimeter first — the contact protocol only works if you detect and channel the approach.

### 8.3. Community & Decadal Flourishing
* **Apprenticeship:** Every adult should participate in at least two contact drills per year. The Speaker role should rotate among capable individuals to prevent a single "diplomat" becoming irreplaceable.
* **Ethical Foundation:** The community must agree: lethal force is a last resort used only when life is directly threatened. This principle is not just ethical — it is strategic. A settlement known for killing strangers will eventually face coordinated retaliation.
