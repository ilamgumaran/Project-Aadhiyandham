# Rationale and Importance: Active Threat Response

**Alignment:** Outcome 3: Perimeter Sovereignty and Passive Defense

## 1. Why This Section Exists

Passive defense (Section 07) handles the majority of security needs through landscape design and early warning. However, a complete perimeter sovereignty framework must also address scenarios where passive measures fail: direct human incursion, organized raiding, and nightwatch rotation protocols. This section will develop non-lethal, de-escalation-first response protocols that protect the community without creating a permanent militarized culture.

## 2. Planned Modules

*   **Nightwatch and Rotation Protocols** - Sustainable watch schedules that prevent burnout while maintaining 24-hour awareness.
*   **De-escalation and Non-Lethal Deterrence** - Communication-first response to human contact, distinguishing travelers from threats.
*   **Emergency Lockdown Procedures** - Rapid community response when passive perimeter alarms are triggered.

## 3. Status

All planned modules have been developed:
*   **02 — Nightwatch and Rotation Protocols** — Sustainable 4-hour shift system for 24-hour perimeter awareness.
*   **03 — De-escalation and Non-Lethal Deterrence** — Five-phase contact protocol with communication-first response.
*   **04 — Emergency Lockdown Procedures** — Community-wide 3-minute lockdown sequence with Safe Core designation.
