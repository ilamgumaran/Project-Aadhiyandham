**Alignment:** Outcome 3: Perimeter Sovereignty and Passive Defense

## 1. Why This Is Important

When passive defenses are breached and de-escalation fails, the community needs a rehearsed, automatic response that prioritizes the safety of non-combatants (children, elderly, injured) and the protection of critical resources (water supply, food stores, seed bank, archive). A lockdown is not a battle plan — it is a structured retreat into the settlement's hardened core, buying time for the threat to pass or for the community to evacuate.

## 2. Expected Outcome

A community-wide emergency protocol that can be activated in under 3 minutes, moving all non-combatants to a designated safe room or hardened structure, securing critical supplies, and blocking entry points — all triggered by a single unambiguous signal.

## 4. Assembly Instructions (Step-by-Step)

### 4.1. Step 1: Designate the Safe Core

Identify or build the single most defensible structure in the settlement — the **Safe Core**. Requirements:
- **Stone, earth, or thick timber walls** (resistant to fire).
- **Single entry point** that can be barricaded from inside.
- **Ventilation** that cannot be blocked from outside (elevated chimney or concealed air shaft).
- **Capacity** for the entire community to shelter for 48 hours.

If no such structure exists, the first construction priority after basic shelter is a root cellar or earthen bunker that doubles as the Safe Core.

### 4.2. Step 2: Pre-Position Barricade Materials

At every entry point to the settlement (trails, gates, gaps in the living wall), pre-position:
- 2 heavy logs that can be dropped or rolled into place as barriers.
- Rope lashings to secure them to anchor posts.
- A sand bucket for fire suppression.

These materials must be accessible in under 30 seconds. Drill it.

### 4.3. Step 3: Define the Lockdown Signal

The lockdown signal must be:
- **Unambiguous:** Cannot be confused with any other signal in the alert escalation ladder.
- **Audible everywhere:** Reachable from any point in the settlement within seconds.

**Standard:** Continuous horn blast (10+ seconds), repeated three times with 5-second gaps. This is the only signal that means "all personnel to Safe Core immediately."

### 4.4. Step 4: Assign Lockdown Roles

Every community member has a pre-assigned role during lockdown:

| Role | Personnel | Action |
|:---|:---|:---|
| **Shepherds** | 2-3 adults | Physically guide children, elderly, and injured to Safe Core |
| **Barricaders** | 2-4 adults | Drop barriers at entry points, then fall back to Safe Core |
| **Supply Runners** | 1-2 adults | Grab the emergency seed bank, medical kit, and archive box |
| **Fire Watch** | 1-2 adults | Position at Safe Core with sand buckets and wet hides |
| **Rear Guard** | 2-3 adults | Last to enter Safe Core; confirm all personnel are accounted for; seal the entry |

### 4.5. Step 5: The Lockdown Sequence

```text
 T+0:00  Continuous horn blast (lockdown signal)
 T+0:30  Shepherds moving non-combatants to Safe Core
 T+1:00  Barricaders dropping entry-point barriers
 T+1:30  Supply Runners arrive at Safe Core with critical items
 T+2:00  Fire Watch in position
 T+2:30  Rear Guard conducts headcount
 T+3:00  Safe Core sealed. All personnel accounted for.
```

**Target: Full lockdown in under 3 minutes.** This requires monthly drills.

### 4.6. Step 6: Inside the Safe Core

Once sealed:
1. **Headcount.** Confirm every community member is present. If someone is missing, do NOT send a rescue party until the threat is assessed.
2. **Silence.** Minimize noise. Listen to the external environment.
3. **Ration.** Begin water and food rationing immediately — assume 48 hours minimum.
4. **Observe.** If the Safe Core has a concealed observation point (peephole, elevated vent), assign one person to monitor.
5. **Wait.** The hardest discipline. Do not open the Safe Core until the all-clear signal is given by two independent observers.

## 6. Vocabulary of the Foundation

* **Safe Core:** The single most defensible structure in the settlement, designated as the community shelter during emergencies.
* **Lockdown Sequence:** A timed, rehearsed series of actions triggered by a single signal, moving all personnel and critical supplies to safety.
* **Rear Guard:** The last personnel to enter the Safe Core, responsible for confirming full headcount and sealing the entry.

## 7. Scientific Validation & Research Context

* **Emergency Evacuation Research:** Studies of building evacuations (Gwynne et al., 1999) show that rehearsed occupants evacuate 3-5x faster than unrehearsed ones. Monthly drills are not optional — they are the mechanism that makes the 3-minute target achievable.
* **Siege Survivability:** Historical analysis of medieval siege warfare confirms that defenders with pre-positioned water, food, and a single defensible entry point can withstand forces many times their number for extended periods. The Safe Core applies this principle at settlement scale.
* **Fire as Primary Threat:** In asymmetric conflicts, fire (not direct assault) is the most common weapon used against fixed positions. The fire watch role and wet-hide protocol directly address the historical primary cause of settlement destruction.

## 8. Practical Implementation Guide for Beginners

### 8.1. Step-by-Step Action Plan
* **Preparation:** Designate the Safe Core and stock it with emergency supplies. Print (or carve) the lockdown roles and post them in every shelter so everyone knows their assignment.
* **Execution:** Run the first lockdown drill in daylight with advance warning. Time it. Run the second drill unannounced. Run the third drill at night. Target: under 3 minutes by the fourth drill.
* **Review:** After each drill, debrief. Where did people get confused? Who was slowest? Which barricade was hardest to deploy? Fix the weak links.

### 8.2. Troubleshooting & Failure Modes
* **Children Panic During Drills:** Frame early drills as a game for young children. Pair each child with a specific adult Shepherd who is responsible for them in every drill and every real lockdown.
* **Safe Core Overheats / Runs Out of Air:** If the Safe Core is a sealed underground space, ventilation testing must be done before any real lockdown. Light a candle inside the sealed space — if it gutters within 2 hours, ventilation is inadequate.
* **Someone Is Missing After Headcount:** The Rear Guard marks the missing person's name and last known location. Do not break lockdown. If the person is injured outside, a rescue can only be attempted after the threat is confirmed resolved.

### 8.3. Community & Decadal Flourishing
* **Apprenticeship:** Children as young as 8 should know the lockdown signal and their assigned Shepherd. By 14, they should be training for Barricader or Supply Runner roles.
* **Psychological Resilience:** Drills must be followed by a community debrief that includes emotional processing. Lockdown drills can trigger anxiety, especially in trauma survivors. Section 08 (The Mind) protocols should be integrated into post-drill recovery.
