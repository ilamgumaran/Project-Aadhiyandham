1. The Botanical Arsenal

Mastering the preparation of these 5 plants provides a primary defense against infection.

How-To: Build a Botanical Press

To preserve medicinal potency, herbs must be dried quickly under pressure:

  • Design: Two flat hardwood boards with corner bolts.
  • The Sandwich: Layer cardboard, paper, and herbs to wick away moisture.
  • Usage: Tighten wingnuts daily as the plants dry to prevent mold and loss of oils.

Preparations

  • Garlic Extract: Must be raw/crushed to activate Allicin. Use for internal infections.
  • Thyme Infusion: Strong tea used as a gargle or tool disinfectant.
  • Echinacea Tincture: Submerge roots in high-proof alcohol for 4 weeks to boost immunity.
  • Sage Decoction: Boil leaves for 10 mins; essential for oral health.
  • Plantain Poultice: Chew clean leaves into a pulp for immediate field treatment of stings/cuts.

Wound Sanitation

  1. Flush with boiled, cooled water.
  2. Apply Thyme or Garlic disinfectant.
  3. Seal with clean linen.

🚀 Practical Implementation Guide for Beginners

1. Step-by-Step Action Plan

  • Preparation: Read this module fully before attempting to build or implement. Gather raw materials locally.
  • Execution: Build a small-scale prototype first to test physics and material strength.
  • Review: Test the implementation under stress (wind, water, heavy use) and refine.

2. Troubleshooting & Failure Modes

  • System Failure: When a system breaks, apply the "5 Whys" technique to find the root cause. Fix the root cause, not just the symptom.
  • Burnout: Decadal survival is a marathon. If exhausted, focus on rest and social cohesion.

3. Community & Decadal Flourishing

  • Apprenticeship: No critical skill should be held by only one person. Document local modifications in a physical journal.
