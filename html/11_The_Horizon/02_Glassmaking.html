1. The Gateway to Lenses

Mastering glass synthesis from local sand and ash transforms the settlement into a scientific civilization.

Pot Furnace Design

How-To: Build Double-Lung Bellows

To reach 1300°C, a continuous, non-pulsing air blast is required:

  • Design: Two independent air chambers connected to a single Y-pipe.
  • Timing: Synchronized so one chamber fills while the other empties.
  • Benefit: Prevents the crucible from cooling between strokes, ensuring a steady melt.

The Glass Batch

  • 3 Parts Sand: Pure white quartz sand, washed and ground.
  • 1 Part Flux: Potash leached from hardwood ash.
  • 0.5 Parts Lime: Crushed limestone or eggshells.

Step-by-Step Melt

  1. Charge: Place batch into the crucible.
  2. Melt: Maintain 1300°C for 12–24 hours using bellows.
  3. Anneal: Cool slowly over 24 hours in a secondary oven to prevent shattering.

🚀 Practical Implementation Guide for Beginners

Detailed Action Plan

  • Phase 1: The Blueprint: Read the theoretical archives fully. Translate the concept into a physical drawing using charcoal or pencil before touching any raw materials.
  • Phase 2: The Prototype: Build a 1/10th scale model of the structure or system. Identify where the stress points are. If the model fails, the real thing will kill you.
  • Phase 3: Redundancy: Never rely on a single system for heat, water, or food. If you build a mechanical system, keep manual hand-tools preserved and oiled as a fallback.

Advanced Troubleshooting

  • The "5 Whys" Method: When a system fails, ask "Why?" five times to reach the root cause. (e.g., The wall collapsed. Why? The wood rotted. Why? Water pooled. Why? No drainage trench. Fix the trench, not just the wall.)
