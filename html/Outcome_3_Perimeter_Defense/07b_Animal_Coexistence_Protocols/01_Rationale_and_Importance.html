1. Why This Section Exists

The most frequent perimeter breaches in any rural settlement come from wildlife, not humans. Bears, wolves, boar, and large herbivores can destroy food stores, injure livestock, and create panic. This section will develop species-specific coexistence protocols that protect both the community and the local wildlife population, maintaining the ecological harmony required by Outcome 2.

2. Planned Modules

  • Large Predator Management - Species-specific deterrence for bears, wolves, and large cats without lethal trapping.
  • Crop Raider Defense - Physical and acoustic barriers for deer, boar, and primates targeting food gardens.
  • Livestock Guardian Systems - Integrating guardian animals (dogs, donkeys, llamas) with passive perimeter defense.

3. Status

All planned modules have been developed:

  • 02 — Large Predator Management — Species-specific non-lethal deterrence for bears, wolves, and large cats.
  • 03 — Crop Raider Defense — Layered physical, sensory, and strategic planting barriers against deer, boar, rabbits, and birds.
  • 04 — Livestock Guardian Systems — Integration of guardian dogs, donkeys, and llamas with passive perimeter defense.
