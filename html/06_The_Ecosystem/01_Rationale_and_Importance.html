Agriculture is the engine of civilization. In the Aadhiyandham model, sustenance must be achieved without reliance on synthetic chemicals or global transport.

Why These Steps are Taken

1. Soil Sovereignty (Terra Preta)

Importance: Soil is a living biological battery.

Rationale: Standard agriculture depletes nutrients. Terra Preta creates a stable, carbon-rich foundation that grows more fertile the more it is used.

2. Genetic Heritage (Heirloom Seeds)

Importance: Biological continuity.

Rationale: We exclusively use open-pollinated heirloom seeds to ensure the settlement can save and adapt crops to its specific microclimate.

3. High-Density Staples

Importance: Efficient land and energy use.

Rationale: We focus on crops like Taro and Potatoes that provide the highest calorie yield per acre with the lowest mechanical input.

4. Livestock Symbiosis

Importance: Mechanical advantage and nutrient cycling.

Rationale: Animals are fertilizer factories and low-tech "tractors," creating a closed-loop system.
