A searchable guide for the moment the plan stops working.

Companion: pressure_test_report.md — the audit these scenarios came from.

> How to use this index. Find your question (search the page for a keyword: water, injured, occupied, checkpoint, bored, winter…), read the first moves, then go to the deep module. And hold one thing in mind while you do: this index does not think for you — it gets you to a starting point. Every entry is a rehearsed hypothesis, not a script; your ground, your people, and your own tested judgment outrank this page the moment they disagree with it. That is not a disclaimer, it is the method of the whole manual: read → practice → observe → revise. The traveler who has rehearsed ten of these scenarios will handle the eleventh — the one this index missed — better than any book could specify. That traveler is the goal, not this list.

A. Before Departure

What if… First moves Go deeper
…society is functioning normally — is this whole plan pointless? No — it works best now: build legally, gradually, with hardware stores and doctors still available. Every skill here improves life under normal law. Start with the 15-minute practices and a garden, not a bug-out bag. Pressure Test §2, Vision
…I would be traveling completely alone? Halve your acceptable risk, hold load to 15% of body weight, plan concealment-first camps, leave a route plan with someone at both ends. Party Playbooks §4
…my party includes small children? Plan 8–15 km/day on the child's numbers, one adult always unloaded enough to carry a child, one voice (the Guardian) for the children, halt-day triggers pre-agreed. Playbooks §6, Family Transit Protocol
…my party includes elderly or infirm members? Route flatness over shortness; inventory every hard-dependency medication first (it may change the whole plan); rig the travois for person-carry; give elders named functional roles. Playbooks §7
…someone depends on medication that will run out? Count the days of supply honestly. That number may argue for going sooner, going nearer, or not going — face the trade at the kitchen table, not at kilometer 80. Playbooks §7 (medication cliff)
…we can't agree on decisions as a group? Decide how you decide before departure: role-holders own routine calls, most-cautious-view wins risk calls, named tie-breakers per domain. Write it into the Party Profile. Playbooks §5
…I've never actually done any of this? Neither has almost anyone. The rehearsal walk (8 km, loaded) and two break-and-fix evenings convert more theory into capability than a month of reading. Schedule them this week. Journey Scenarios rationale, Field Repair
…I don't know when to leave? Pre-commit indicators now, while calm — normalcy bias guarantees "too late" if you decide in the moment. (Framework still open — see the ledger.) Pressure Test §3.3

B. On the Journey

What if… First moves Go deeper
…someone is injured or falls ill mid-route? Halt day, full stop — one early halt beats five forced later. Medic assesses against the carry option (travois converts). Child fever/vomiting is an automatic halt trigger. Playbooks §6–7, Advanced Trauma Care
…we're behind schedule? It's a route-and-rations problem, never a pace problem: shift to the cached/alternate route, adjust rations — do not walk it out of the bodies. Playbooks §8
…we're lost? Stop at the first doubt (every step in doubt doubles the correction). Back-bearing to the last certain point; re-fix from terrain handrails and the sun. Orienting and Positioning, Map and Compass
…footwear fails? Class A: it owns the schedule — stop and fix properly today. Saddle-stitch/resin (kit), tire-sole (scavenge), rawhide carbatina (land). Feet checked at every halt before this happens. Field Repair §4.1
…the travois or cart breaks? Morning lashing audit prevents most of it; spare pole, drag-end shoe, rawhide re-lash fix the rest. Any cart must convert back to a travois. Field Repair §4.3
…we lose a water container? Treat it as a route problem first: re-plan the day to hug water while you craft the replacement (found vessel → bark-and-resin → rawhide bag). Field Repair §4.2, Riverine Logic
…a water source on the route is dry or foul? Fall back to the drainage logic — water runs downhill and concentrates; boil everything regardless of appearance. Re-plan the day around the next two sources, not one. Riverine Logic, Hydrological Analysis
…we see strangers before they see us? Freeze low, observe, let them pass, resume after spacing. Log it — people imply more people. Never shadow them. Low-Profile Travel §5.1
…strangers see us / approach us? Stop, brief empty hands, alter course visibly, keep unchanged pace. If talk is unavoidable: one Speaker, boring script, bounded generosity, end it first and politely. Low-Profile Travel §5.2–5.3
…there's a checkpoint or controlled crossing ahead? Observe from cover 30–60 min before being seen, then run the decision ladder: detour (default) → present openly by day → withdraw → comply-and-bore. Never approach at night. Low-Profile Travel §5.4
…an encounter turns hostile? It's a walk, not a fight: dependents first to the pre-named rally point, Rear Guard last, Speaker filling the air. Coherent, calm groups are almost never pursued. Low-Profile Travel §5.5, De-escalation
…we get separated? Execute the double rendezvous (primary point + time, fallback +24 h) — which exists because you agreed it aloud before any split. No freelance searching. Playbooks §5
…we must camp in inhabited country? Dusk-camp doctrine: cook at site one near water, walk 300–800 m, sleep cold and dark at site two, off-path with two exits. Low-Profile Travel §6
…food runs out en route? Ration to the map, not the stomach: the packing margin plus emergency wild staples (acorn, cattail) bridge to arrival. Nobody forages while exhausted or in the dark. Emergency Nutrition, Essential Packing List
…severe weather is coming? Read it 12–24 h out (cloud sequence, wind shift, pressure signs) and spend the margin early: shelter high-and-dry before it arrives, not during. Micro-Climate Forecasting, Phenological Indicators

C. Arrival

What if… First moves Go deeper
…the site is already occupied? Do not contest, do not camp adjacent. Withdraw unseen to the overwatch halt; choose: fallback site, observed open parley (only from strength of information), or long detour. Arrival Protocol §3
…the site doesn't match the dossier (water failed, burned, flooded)? Decide at the overwatch halt with daylight in hand, not at dusk inside the ruin. The fallback dossier — chosen before departure — is what makes this a decision instead of a disaster. Arrival Protocol §3, Candidate Atlas
…everyone is too exhausted to do arrival "properly"? The fatigue override is proper: water boiled, vulnerable sheltered, noise ground on two approaches — then sleep. Day two exists. Schedule day three as the rest inventory, because the collapse is coming anyway. Arrival Protocol §4–6
…the water looks perfectly clean? Boil it anyway. Clear is not clean, the dossier is old, and week-one waterborne illness is the most likely lethal event of the whole project. Arrival Protocol §4, Bio-Sand Filtration
…it's autumn and winter is coming? The hardest honest answer in the manual: without ~6 months' food margin, overwinter where supply exists and travel in spring. Arriving hungry into a dead season is the historical settlement-killer. Pressure Test §5.2–5.3, Long-Term Food Preservation
…something moves outside camp at night? Presence and noise, not stealth: dependents to the rally point, ables converge with light and voice. Nearly every night alarm is an animal, and that response handles both. Arrival Protocol §5, Large Predator Management

D. The First Year

What if… First moves Go deeper
…the first crop underperforms badly? It usually does (30–60% of plan is the historical norm). This is what seed diversity, wild-staple knowledge, and the food margin were for — deploy all three and adjust the second planting from the first's data. Pressure Test §5.3, Emergency Nutrition, Heirloom Seed Banking
…people start getting stomach illness? Assume your own water/sanitation loop first — it is almost always the latrine, the hands, or the "clean" source. Re-verify the chain end to end; boil everything until found. Immediate Sanitation, Thermophilic Sanitation Math
…serious injury or a difficult birth happens here, without a hospital? The trauma and midwifery protocols are the best zero-tech medicine available — and the manual owes you honesty: some events they cannot save. Prevention culture (sanitation, dental, no risky solo work) is where the real mortality reduction lives. Advanced Trauma Care, Midwifery and Childbirth, Pressure Test §5.5
…predators or crop raiders find the fields? Layered coexistence, not eradication: exclusion for the garden core, guardians and deterrence for the ring, tolerance at the edge. Crop Raider Defense, Livestock Guardian Systems
…wildfire threatens the site? The defensible space you cut in month one is the plan; ignition discipline and the fire-break ring are maintenance, not crisis response. Wildfire Defensible Space
…outsiders discover the settlement? First contact is a protocol, not an improvisation: observation, graduated disclosure, bounded generosity — the settlement-side versions of the road doctrine. First Contact Protocols, De-escalation
…a group demands entry or tribute? Lockdown posture buys time; de-escalation and visible not-worth-it-ness do the work. Passive defense buys cost and delay, never immunity — remoteness and alliances are the real wall. Emergency Lockdown, Pressure Test §6.2
…two members are in serious conflict? Treat it as seriously as a breached perimeter — internal conflict dissolves more settlements than raiders ever have. Mediation protocol, early, with the full circle behind it. Conflict Resolution, Power Vacuum Prevention
…someone wants to leave? Free exit, honored well: forced retention poisons everything the settlement is for. Provision them, record what they carry of the archive, keep the door described as open. Group Dynamics rationale, Integration of Newcomers
…grief overwhelms the community after a death? Grief is processed as a community function, not a private malfunction — the rituals exist for exactly this load. Collective Grief Processing, Natural Burial

E. The Decade and Beyond

What if… First moves Go deeper
…scavenged tools and materials are running out? This was scheduled: the Recycling Cliff is a curve you climb before it arrives — bloomery, ceramics, cordage, textiles started in year 2–5, not year 19. Recycling Cliff, Material Synthesis
…the only person who knows a critical skill dies? Too late to prevent, so prevent it now: every master has an apprentice, audited annually. After the loss: the archive and the network are the recovery path. Pressure Test §7.2, The Archive rationale, Knowledge Exchange
…the settlement is too small to be viable long-term? It is — every single settlement is. The inter-settlement network (partners, genes, knowledge, mutual defense) is not diplomacy garnish; it is the survival requirement. Budget real effort toward contact. Genetic Diversity Exchange, Pressure Test §7.1
…the climate itself is shifting under the site? The site was chosen with thresholds, not just averages — re-run them honestly every few years and treat relocation of fields (or the settlement) as an engineering decision, not a heresy. Climate Adaptation Thresholds
…our practices are hardening into ritual and rules nobody questions? The 50–75 year dogma cycle, arriving on schedule. The antidote is structural: Revision Council, falsifiability teaching, evidence-outranks-status — used, not displayed. The Scientific Method
…governance is drifting toward a strongman or a faction? Power vacuums and crystallizing hierarchies are named failure modes with named counters — rotation, transparency, distributed keys to critical systems. Power Vacuum Prevention, Consensus Models

F. The Mind (any phase)

What if… First moves Go deeper
…I'm afraid, and the fear won't stop? Body first: extended-exhale breathing, cold water on the face, then the dichotomy-of-control sort — the sequence works during the event, not just after. Psychological Grounding
…survival is handled and I feel… bored? Restless? Empty? This is the project succeeding — freed attention with nowhere yet to go. The boredom ladder: sit with it ten minutes, walk with one question, observe one square meter, open the inner laboratory. Raja Yoga: The Inner Laboratory, Cultural Technology
…I want to go deeper than daily grounding — actually investigate my own mind? The eight-limbed program, run as first-person experiments with a lab notebook: seat, breath, withdrawal, one-pointed attention. Take nothing on trust, including the manual. Raja Yoga: The Inner Laboratory
…meditation practice itself becomes doctrine, hierarchy, or escape? Named failure modes with counters: no orthodoxy, no exemptions from community accountability, gentle shared forms in crisis, the Scientific Method's rules applied to inner life. Raja Yoga §6–7
…someone's mind is genuinely breaking — not bored, not grieving, but ill? Compassionate structure, safety, and continuity of care lead; contemplative practice steps back to gentle, accompanied forms only. Mental Illness and Compassionate Care
…I've lost the sense that any of this matters? Meaning is infrastructure, and it is buildable: ritual, shared work, play, and the inner laboratory are the four load-bearing walls. Start with the one nearest to hand tonight. Cultural Technology, Architecture of Play, Philosophy and Thought Experiments

The scenario this index missed

There will be one. When you meet it: stop, breathe once the long way out, and run the method every module here runs — what do I observe (not assume)? what does it threaten first (water > warmth > safety > food)? what's the cheapest reversible move? Make that move, observe again, and write down what happened. Then — when there is time — add the scenario to this index for the next traveler. That final step is not optional housekeeping; it is how a manual stays alive, and how you repay the strangers who wrote the entries that got you this far.
