Candidate Location Evaluation: Daintree Lowlands, Australia


Location Overview

  • **Name:** Daintree Lowlands (Coastal Mountain Foothills)
  • **Coordinates/Region:** Far North Queensland, Australia
  • **GPS Coordinates:** 16.1056° S, 145.4470° E
  • **Base Elevation:** ~100m - 300m
  • **Primary Biome:** Tropical Lowland Rainforest

    • Phase I: Placement & Arrival (Pass/Fail Criteria)

  • [x] **Elevation:** 100m+ above sea level? Yes.
  • [x] **Protection:** Shielded by steep coastal mountains? Yes. Traps orographic moisture and buffers winds.
  • [x] **Gravity Water:** Year-round water sources from higher elevation? Yes. Massive rainfall and reliable mountain streams.
  • **Notes:** One of the most stable rainforest refugia on Earth (100M+ years of continuity).

    • Phase II: Stabilization & Sustenance

  • **Solar Potential:** Moderate to High. Consistent tropical sun, slightly buffered by cloud cover.
  • **Soil Type:** Ancient metamorphic and granitic forest loams. High organic matter.
  • **Biomass Availability:** Extreme. Dense primary forest and high growth rates.
  • **Agricultural Score (8.5/10):** Elite for tropical fruits and nuts.

    • Phase III: The Human System

  • **Navigability:** Foothills are navigable; river systems provide corridors.
  • **Climate & Mood:** Warm and vibrant. The ancient forest environment is profoundly restorative.
  • **Health Score (7.5/10):** Exceptional air and water quality.

    • Phase IV & V: Wisdom & Synthesis

  • **Geological Foundations:** Abundant stone and river gravels. Ancient Kuku Yalanji resource management template.
  • **Isolation vs. Network:** Isolated North QLD location; defensible via the range and dense forest.

    • Final Verdict

  • **Recommendation:** **PROCEED (Elite Tropical Candidate)**
  • **Justification:** The ultimate "biological lifeboat." Offers extreme stability and infinite water/biomass resources.

    • 🚀 Practical Implementation Guide for Beginners

    Detailed Action Plan
