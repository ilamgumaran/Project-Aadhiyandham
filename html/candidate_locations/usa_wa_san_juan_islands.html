Candidate Location Evaluation: San Juan Islands, WA (USA)


Location Overview

  • **Name:** San Juan Islands (Low Benches)
  • **Coordinates/Region:** Salish Sea, Washington State, USA
  • **Base Elevation:** ~10m - 100m (Benches) to 700m (Peaks)
  • **Primary Biome:** Coastal Douglas Fir / Garry Oak Savanna

    • Phase I: Placement & Arrival (Pass/Fail Criteria)

  • [x] **Elevation:** 100m+ above sea level? **FAIL (Benches are low)**. *Note: Island peaks provide safety, but core living is low.*
  • [x] **Protection:** Shielded by major ranges? Yes. Nestled in the double rain shadow of the Olympics and Vancouver Island.
  • [x] **Gravity Water:** Year-round water sources from higher elevation? **LIMITED**. No major rivers; relies on local precipitation catchment and limited mountain springs.
  • **Notes:** The "Banana Belt" of the Northwest; unique marine stability and sunshine.

    • Phase II: Stabilization & Sustenance

  • **Solar Potential:** Very High (for the region). Over 240 days of sunshine; highest solar yield in Western Washington.
  • **Soil Type:** Glacial outwash and thin volcanic soils. Requires intensive organic matter (Module 02) to manage water in the dry shadow.
  • **Biomass Availability:** Moderate to High. Douglas fir and Garry oak; extensive marine biomass (seaweed/shellfish).
  • **Agricultural Score (7.5/10):** Elite for berries, orchard fruits, and brassicas. Water scarcity is the primary limiting factor.

    • Phase III: The Human System

  • **Navigability:** Excellent. The low benches and rolling hills are ideal for all non-electric transport.
  • **Climate & Mood:** Peaceful and stable. The maritime influence prevents extremes, supporting a calm "centered null" state.
  • **Health Score (9/10):** Cleanest air/water in the region. High biosecurity due to island isolation.

    • Phase IV & V: Wisdom & Synthesis

  • **Geological Foundations:** Abundant stone, sand, and marine resources. Proximity to Puget Sound ruins for salvage.
  • **Isolation vs. Network:** Naturally defensible via sea. A historical center of Coast Salish resource management (Camas prairies).

    • Final Verdict

  • **Recommendation:** **MONITOR (Water Security Exception)**
  • **Justification:** While breaking the 100m altitude and gravity-water rules, the San Juans offer a unique maritime stability and solar yield. For a society focusing on marine synthesis and rain-shadow agriculture, it is a premier refugia.
