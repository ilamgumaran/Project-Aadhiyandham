Candidate Location Evaluation: Fouta Djallon Terraces, Guinea (Africa)


Location Overview

  • **Name:** Fouta Djallon Terraces
  • **Coordinates/Region:** Central Guinea (Labé / Dalaba)
  • **GPS Coordinates:** 11.3172° N, 12.2872° W
  • **Base Elevation:** ~300m (Lower valleys and terraces) - *Note: Plateau reaches 1,500m.*
  • **Primary Biome:** Guinean Forest-Savanna Mosaic

    • Phase I: Placement & Arrival (Pass/Fail Criteria)

  • [x] **Elevation:** 100m+ above sea level? Yes.
  • [x] **Protection:** No major range shield (it is the range). Internal protection via deep valleys and escarpments.
  • [x] **Gravity Water:** Year-round water sources from higher elevation? Yes. The **"Water Tower of West Africa"**; source of the Niger, Senegal, and Gambia rivers.
  • **Notes:** High climate stability; historically a refuge during Saharan aridity events.

    • Phase II: Stabilization & Sustenance

  • **Solar Potential:** High. Consistent tropical solar gain, slightly buffered by cloud cover in the rainy season.
  • **Soil Type:** **Ferralitic and acidic.** Prone to forming hard "bowal" caps. Requires intensive biochar (Module 02) and terracing to unlock fertility.
  • **Biomass Availability:** High. Diverse forest types providing timber, cashew, mango, and avocado resources.
  • **Agricultural Score (7.5/10):** Exceptional for fonio, rice, and tropical fruits. Requires sophisticated water management (terracing) to prevent soil erosion on slopes.

    • Phase III: The Human System

  • **Navigability:** Valleys are navigable; plateau slopes require animal power or well-engineered footpaths.
  • **Climate & Mood:** Stable and temperate (due to altitude). Known for its "eternal spring" like quality compared to the surrounding lowlands.
  • **Health Score (8/10):** Superior water quality at the source. High altitude reduces the prevalence of some tropical disease vectors.

    • Phase IV & V: Wisdom & Synthesis

  • **Geological Foundations:** Abundant granite and volcanic stones. High-quality bauxite and iron deposits in the wider region.
  • **Isolation vs. Network:** Naturally defensible via high escarpments. A historical center of knowledge and Islamic scholarship in West Africa.

    • Final Verdict

  • **Recommendation:** **PROCEED (Top African Hydrological Candidate)**
  • **Justification:** The Fouta Djallon is the primary water security site for West Africa. For a society prioritizing hydrological sovereignty and a stable, high-altitude tropical climate, this is the premier candidate.
