Candidate Location Evaluation: Willamette Foothills, Oregon (USA)


Location Overview

  • **Name:** Willamette Foothills (Jory Soil Belt)
  • **Coordinates/Region:** Willamette Valley periphery (Yamhill, Polk, and Marion counties), Oregon
  • **GPS Coordinates:** 44.9429° N, 123.0351° W
  • **Base Elevation:** ~100m - 300m (Foothills)
  • **Primary Biome:** Temperate Broadleaf and Mixed Forest

    • Phase I: Placement & Arrival (Pass/Fail Criteria)

  • [x] **Elevation:** 100m+ above sea level? Yes (benches above the valley floor).
  • [x] **Protection:** Shielded by a major mountain range? Yes. Nestled between the Coast Range (West) and the Cascades (East).
  • [x] **Gravity Water:** Year-round water sources from higher elevation? Yes. Fed by numerous perennial streams flowing from the Cascades and Coast Range.
  • **Notes:** Historically high water security, though reliance on snowpack is shifting toward winter rain dominance.

    • Phase II: Stabilization & Sustenance

  • **Solar Potential:** Moderate. High winter cloud cover, but excellent summer solar gain.
  • **Soil Type:** **Jory Soils** (weathered basalt). Deep, well-drained, nutrient-rich reddish silty clay loams. Among the best agricultural soils in North America.
  • **Biomass Availability:** Extremely High. Dense forests of Douglas fir, Bigleaf maple, and Oregon white oak.
  • **Agricultural Score (9/10):** Elite for "dry farming" (tomatoes, squash), hazelnuts, berries, and viticulture. Excellent for silvopasture and temperate hardwood production.

    • Phase III: The Human System

  • **Navigability:** Rolling hills are easily navigable by foot, bicycle, and horse.
  • **Climate & Mood:** Mild, temperate, and green. The "evergreen" environment is highly restorative, though long grey winters require intentional architectural light management (Module 06).
  • **Health Score (8.5/10):** High availability of medicinal plants (elderberry, nettle, Oregon grape). Low humidity reduces heat-related stress.

    • Phase IV & V: Wisdom & Synthesis

  • **Geological Foundations:** Abundant basalt (for building) and river clays. Proximity to Cascade mineral resources.
  • **Isolation vs. Network:** Protected by mountains but close enough to the ruins of Portland/Salem for salvage. Strong local heritage of sustainable agriculture and land-use protection.

    • Final Verdict

  • **Recommendation:** **PROCEED (Top Tier Temperate Candidate)**
  • **Justification:** The Willamette Foothills combine world-class soil fertility with high water security and a mild climate. It is an ideal site for a society focusing on long-term agricultural stability and rapid biomass growth.

    • 🚀 Practical Implementation Guide for Beginners

    Detailed Action Plan

    • Phase 1: The Blueprint: Read the theoretical archives fully. Translate the concept into a physical drawing using charcoal or pencil before touching any raw materials.
    • Phase 2: The Prototype: Build a 1/10th scale model of the structure or system. Identify where the stress points are. If the model fails, the real thing will kill you.
    • Phase 3: Redundancy: Never rely on a single system for heat, water, or food. If you build a mechanical system, keep manual hand-tools preserved and oiled as a fallback.

    Advanced Troubleshooting

    • The "5 Whys" Method: When a system fails, ask "Why?" five times to reach the root cause. (e.g., The wall collapsed. Why? The wood rotted. Why? Water pooled. Why? No drainage trench. Fix the trench, not just the wall.)
