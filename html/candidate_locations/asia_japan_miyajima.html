Candidate Location Evaluation: Miyajima Island, Japan


Location Overview

  • **Name:** Miyajima Island (Lower Slopes of Mt. Misen)
  • **Coordinates/Region:** Hiroshima Prefecture, Seto Inland Sea, Japan
  • **GPS Coordinates:** 34.2908° N, 132.3185° E
  • **Base Elevation:** ~100m - 300m (Slopes above the shrine area)
  • **Primary Biome:** Temperate Evergreen and Broadleaf Forest

    • Phase I: Placement & Arrival (Pass/Fail Criteria)

  • [x] **Elevation:** 100m+ above sea level? Yes.
  • [x] **Protection:** Shielded by the Seto Inland Sea? Yes. The island geography and Mt. Misen provide a stable maritime microclimate.
  • [x] **Gravity Water:** Year-round water sources from higher elevation? Yes. Permanent springs and streams flow from the peaks.
  • **Notes:** Protected as a "sacred forest" for over 1,000 years; extreme ecological stability.

    • Phase II: Stabilization & Sustenance

  • **Solar Potential:** High. Stable maritime-influenced sunshine.
  • **Soil Type:** Rich, mineral-dense granitic forest loams. High fertility.
  • **Biomass Availability:** Moderate to High. Intact primary forest provides diverse timber and medicinal resources.
  • **Agricultural Score (7.5/10):** Elite for cool-temperate fruits and vegetables. Limited flat land for grains.

    • Phase III: The Human System

  • **Navigability:** Lower slopes are navigable; steeper interior provides defensive isolation.
  • **Climate & Mood:** Temperate and tranquil. The "sacred" aesthetic is profoundly restorative.
  • **Health Score (9/10):** Exceptional air and water quality.

    • Phase IV & V: Wisdom & Synthesis

  • **Geological Foundations:** Abundant granite for building. Ancient history of Japanese resource management and craftsmanship.
  • **Isolation vs. Network:** Isolated island location; defensible via distance and terrain.

    • Final Verdict

  • **Recommendation:** **PROCEED (Top Tier Island Refugia)**
  • **Justification:** A unique "sacred refugium" providing extreme stability and biosecurity in a stable maritime-influenced region.
