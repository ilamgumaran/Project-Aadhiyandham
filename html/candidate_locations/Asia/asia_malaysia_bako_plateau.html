Candidate Location Evaluation: Bako Plateau, Sarawak (Asia)


**Alignment:** Outcome 1: Locating and Connecting Optimal Refugia


1. Location Overview

  • **Name:** Bako Plateau
  • **Coordinates/Region:** Sarawak, Malaysia (Borneo)
  • **GPS Coordinates:** 1.7167° N, 110.4667° E
  • **Base Elevation:** ~100m - 260m
  • **Primary Biome:** Kerangas Forest (Heath Forest)

    • 2. Phase I: Placement & Arrival (Pass/Fail Criteria)

  • [x] **Elevation:** 100m+ above sea level? Yes.
  • [x] **Protection:** Shielded by sandstone cliffs? Yes. The plateau is a natural fortress rising from the South China Sea.
  • [x] **Gravity Water:** Year-round water sources from higher elevation? Yes (from internal solution pans and springs).
  • **Notes:** A unique "ecological island" with extreme biosecurity and topographical protection.

    • 3. Phase II: Stabilization & Sustenance

  • **Solar Potential:** High. Unlike the surrounding rainforest, the plateau is exposed and sunny.
  • **Soil Type:** **Kerangas (Podzols).** Highly acidic, sandy, and nutrient-poor. Requires intensive Terra Preta (Module 02) management to support food crops.
  • **Biomass Availability:** Moderate. Stunted forest provides specialized timbers; surrounding mangroves offer abundant marine biomass.
  • **Agricultural Score (6.5/10):** Difficult. Requires sophisticated soil synthesis. Elite for specific medicinal plants and high-value carnivorous species.

    • 4. Phase III: The Human System

  • **Navigability:** Flat sandstone plateau is easy to navigate by foot; cliffs provide natural boundaries.
  • **Climate & Mood:** Hot and well-ventilated. The combination of ocean breeze and high altitude supports a clear, alert mental state.
  • **Health Score (8/10):** High biosecurity due to isolated "pocket" geography. Clean air and saltwater exposure.

    • 5. Phase IV & V: Wisdom & Synthesis

  • **Geological Foundations:** Abundant sandstone and marine resources. Proximity to Sarawak ruins for salvage potential.
  • **Isolation vs. Network:** The ultimate defensive position. Reachable only by sea or narrow cliff paths. A "natural citadel."

    • 6. Final Verdict

  • **Recommendation:** **PROCEED (Specialized Defensive Candidate)**
  • **Justification:** For a society prioritizing biosecurity and defensive isolation in the tropics, Bako is elite. While soil fertility requires active synthesis, its topographical security is unmatched in Southeast Asia.

    7. 7.Practical Implementation Guide for Beginners


    7.1. Step-by-Step Action Plan

  • **Preparation:** Read this module fully before attempting to build or implement. Gather raw materials locally.
  • **Execution:** Build a small-scale prototype first. For example, if building a mechanical system or shelter, make a miniature version to test physics and material strength.
  • **Review:** Test the implementation under stress (wind, water, heavy use) and refine.

    • 7.2. Troubleshooting & Failure Modes

  • **System Failure:** When a system breaks, apply the "5 Whys" technique to find the root cause (e.g., The gear broke. Why? It was made of soft wood. Why? We lacked hardwood. Why?). Fix the root cause, not just the symptom.
  • **Burnout:** Decadal survival is a marathon, not a sprint. If the community is exhausted, lower productivity expectations and focus on rest and social cohesion.

    • 7.3. Community & Decadal Flourishing

  • **Apprenticeship:** No critical skill should be held by only one person. Every master must have an apprentice. Document local modifications to these original instructions in a physical journal.
