Candidate Location Evaluation: Resolution Island, New Zealand


**Alignment:** Outcome 1: Locating and Connecting Optimal Refugia


1. Location Overview

  • **Name:** Resolution Island (Lowland Forests)
  • **Coordinates/Region:** Fiordland, South Island, NZ
  • **GPS Coordinates:** 45.6667° S, 166.6667° E
  • **Base Elevation:** ~100m - 300m (Benches above the coastline)
  • **Primary Biome:** Cool Temperate Podocarp-Beech Forest

    • 2. Phase I: Placement & Arrival (Pass/Fail Criteria)

  • [x] **Elevation:** 100m+ above sea level? Yes.
  • [x] **Protection:** Shielded by the Southern Alps? Yes. The deep fjords and mountainous interior provide massive storm buffering.
  • [x] **Gravity Water:** Year-round water sources from higher elevation? Yes. Extreme rainfall feeds permanent springs and streams.
  • **Notes:** Historically stable maritime climate; "pest-free" offshore island status provides extreme biosecurity.

    • 3. Phase II: Stabilization & Sustenance

  • **Solar Potential:** Moderate. High cloud cover and low sun angle in winter.
  • **Soil Type:** Fertile, carbon-rich forest loams over glaciated bedrock.
  • **Biomass Availability:** Extreme. Dense primary forest provides inexhaustible resources.
  • **Agricultural Score (7/10):** Elite for cool-temperate fruits, brassicas, and fungi. Requires specialized greenhouses for grains.

    • 4. Phase III: The Human System

  • **Navigability:** Coastline and lower benches are navigable; the interior is rugged.
  • **Climate & Mood:** Cool, moist, and quiet. The "end of the world" isolation is profoundly peaceful.
  • **Health Score (9.5/10):** Cleanest air/water in the world. Naturally isolated from global pathogens.

    • 5. Phase IV & V: Wisdom & Synthesis

  • **Geological Foundations:** Abundant stone and high-quality timber. Ancient history of Māori maritime resource management.
  • **Isolation vs. Network:** The ultimate island sanctuary. Defensible via "Natural Moat" (the Southern Ocean).

    • 6. Final Verdict

  • **Recommendation:** **PROCEED (Top Tier Island Sanctuary)**
  • **Justification:** The ultimate "safety" pick for Oceania. Its combination of biosecurity, water security, and isolation makes it a primary refugia.

    7. 7.Practical Implementation Guide for Beginners


    7.1. Step-by-Step Action Plan

  • **Preparation:** Read this module fully before attempting to build or implement. Gather raw materials locally.
  • **Execution:** Build a small-scale prototype first. For example, if building a mechanical system or shelter, make a miniature version to test physics and material strength.
  • **Review:** Test the implementation under stress (wind, water, heavy use) and refine.

    • 7.2. Troubleshooting & Failure Modes

  • **System Failure:** When a system breaks, apply the "5 Whys" technique to find the root cause (e.g., The gear broke. Why? It was made of soft wood. Why? We lacked hardwood. Why?). Fix the root cause, not just the symptom.
  • **Burnout:** Decadal survival is a marathon, not a sprint. If the community is exhausted, lower productivity expectations and focus on rest and social cohesion.

    • 7.3. Community & Decadal Flourishing

  • **Apprenticeship:** No critical skill should be held by only one person. Every master must have an apprentice. Document local modifications to these original instructions in a physical journal.
