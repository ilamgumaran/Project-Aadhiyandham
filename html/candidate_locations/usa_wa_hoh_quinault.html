Candidate Location Evaluation: Hoh and Quinault Valleys, USA


Location Overview

  • **Name:** Hoh and Quinault Valleys (Olympic National Park)
  • **Coordinates/Region:** Olympic Peninsula, Washington State
  • **GPS Coordinates:** 47.8607° N, 123.9351° W
  • **Base Elevation:** ~150m - 300m
  • **Primary Biome:** Temperate Rainforest

    • Phase I: Placement & Arrival (Pass/Fail Criteria)

  • [x] **Elevation:** 100m+ above sea level? Yes.
  • [x] **Protection:** Shielded by the massive Olympic range? Yes. The deep valleys provide shelter from maritime storms.
  • [x] **Gravity Water:** Year-round water sources from higher elevation? Yes. Fed by intense rainfall and glacial melt.
  • **Notes:** High "fog drip" maintains humidity; thermally stable maritime-buffered environment.

    • Phase II: Stabilization & Sustenance

  • **Solar Potential:** Moderate. High cloud cover; requires clearing for intensive solar and agriculture.
  • **Soil Type:** Deep, nutrient-rich glacial silts and alluvial forest loams.
  • **Biomass Availability:** Extreme. One of the highest biomass densities on Earth.
  • **Agricultural Score (7.5/10):** Elite for cool-temperate fruits, brassicas, and fungi. Grains require rain-shadow benches (Module 01).

    • Phase III: The Human System

  • **Navigability:** Valley floors are flat and easily traversed.
  • **Climate & Mood:** Moist and green. The "cathedral forest" aesthetic is profoundly restorative.
  • **Health Score (9/10):** Exceptional air and water quality.

    • Phase IV & V: Wisdom & Synthesis

  • **Geological Foundations:** Abundant stone, wood, and river gravels.
  • **Isolation vs. Network:** Naturally isolated by the Olympic range and dense forest.

    • Final Verdict

  • **Recommendation:** **PROCEED (Top Tier Temperate Refuge)**
  • **Justification:** The ultimate water and biomass security site. Provides a stable, protected refuge in a warming world.
