Candidate Location Evaluation: Olympic Peninsula (East Side), WA (USA)


Location Overview

  • **Name:** Olympic Peninsula (East Side / Sequim-Dungeness Valley)
  • **Coordinates/Region:** Clallam and Jefferson counties, Washington State
  • **GPS Coordinates:** 48.0795° N, 123.1018° W
  • **Base Elevation:** ~10m - 100m (Valley floor) to 300m (Foothills)
  • **Primary Biome:** Douglas Fir Forest / Garry Oak Prairie (Rain Shadow)

    • Phase I: Placement & Arrival (Pass/Fail Criteria)

  • [x] **Elevation:** 100m+ above sea level? Yes (foothills and higher terraces).
  • [x] **Protection:** Shielded by a major mountain range? Yes. The Olympic Mountains create a massive rain shadow and wind buffer.
  • [x] **Gravity Water:** Year-round water sources from higher elevation? Yes. Fed by snowmelt from the Olympics (Dungeness River).
  • **Notes:** Unique "Blue Hole" microclimate with significantly less rainfall (16 inches) than the surrounding rainforests. Extremely stable maritime-buffered temperatures.

    • Phase II: Stabilization & Sustenance

  • **Solar Potential:** High (for the region). The rain shadow clears clouds frequently, leading to higher sunshine hours than Seattle or the coast.
  • **Soil Type:** Glacial outwash and fertile river silts. Well-draining; requires organic matter (Module 02) to increase water retention in the dry shadow.
  • **Biomass Availability:** High. Dense coniferous forests (Douglas fir, cedar) provide abundant timber.
  • **Agricultural Score (8/10):** Exceptional for lavender, berries, and brassicas. Irrigation is required due to low rainfall, making gravity-fed water systems critical.

    • Phase III: The Human System

  • **Navigability:** Excellent. The Dungeness and Chimacum valleys are relatively flat and easy to traverse.
  • **Climate & Mood:** "The Mediterranean of the Northwest." Calm, clear, and restorative. High air quality and lack of extreme temperature spikes support mental well-being.
  • **Health Score (9/10):** Clean alpine water and maritime air. Low humidity reduces common Pacific Northwest fungal/mold issues.

    • Phase IV & V: Wisdom & Synthesis

  • **Geological Foundations:** Abundant glacial till, river rock, and clays. Proximity to the coast allows for marine resource harvesting (seaweed, shells for lime).
  • **Isolation vs. Network:** Naturally isolated on a peninsula with limited road access (Hood Canal Bridge/Hwy 101). Highly defensible while maintaining proximity to Puget Sound ruins for salvage.

    • Final Verdict

  • **Recommendation:** **PROCEED (Top Tier Maritime Candidate)**
  • **Justification:** The Olympic East Side is a rare "dry temperate" refugium. It offers the protection of high mountains and the stability of the ocean, with a microclimate that facilitates much higher agricultural productivity and solar gain than its surroundings.
