Water Sovereignty: The Hydraulic Ram Pump

1. Zero-Energy Water Movement

The Hydraulic Ram Pump (Hydram) uses the momentum of falling water to lift a portion of that water uphill with zero external energy.

Mechanical Principle: The Hydraulic Ram Pump

The kinetic energy of falling water is captured by the slamming waste valve, forcing a small portion of water into the air chamber and up the delivery pipe.

Step-by-Step Assembly

  1. Drive Pipe: Install a rigid pipe (Steel/thick PVC) from the source to the pump.
  2. The Engine: Mount the Waste Valve (normally-open) on a heavy iron Tee.
  3. The Buffer: Mount the Pressure Vessel (air chamber) above the Delivery Valve.
  4. Finding the Drum Beat: Push the Waste Valve shut with your foot. It will pop open. Keep pushing it shut every time it opens until it catches the rhythm and starts "clacking" on its own—CLICK-CLACK, CLICK-CLACK.
