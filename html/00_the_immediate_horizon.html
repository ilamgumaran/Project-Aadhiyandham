00 The Immediate Horizon: The First 100 Days

1. Immediate Bio-Security

The first priority upon arrival at a new site is the establishment of water safety and temporary shelter.

Slow Sand Filter (SSF) Construction

Maintain constant flow to keep the biological layer (green) alive.

How-To: Building the Filter

  1. Procure a 2.5m tall tank or build a masonry chimney.
  2. Layer gravel, then biochar, then 0.8m of washed fine sand.
  3. Fill with water and wait 10-14 days for the "Schmutzdecke" (biological film) to ripen.
  4. Crucial: Ensure the outlet pipe rises above the sand level so the sand never dries out.

2. Shelter & Thermal Grounding

Utilize Wattle and Daub for immediate insulative shelter or Rammed Earth for long-term thermal mass.
