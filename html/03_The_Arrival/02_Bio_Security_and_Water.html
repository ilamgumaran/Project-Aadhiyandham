Bio-Security: Water Purification (Slow Sand Filter)

1. Why This Is Important

Pathogens (tiny germs) in untreated water are like an invisible enemy. A Slow Sand Filter (SSF) is a "living" machine. Instead of using chemicals, it grows a layer of "good germs" called the Living Scum (Schmutzdecke) that eats the "bad germs."

2. Expected Outcome

2,000 Liters of safe water per day—enough for 50 people.

Technical Detail: Slow Sand Filter

The biological "Living Scum" consumes pathogens while the sand and biochar provide mechanical and chemical filtration.

Step-by-Step Assembly

  1. The Drain: Put a perforated pipe at the bottom.
  2. Layering: Add gravel (30cm), then Biochar (20cm), then Fine Sand (80cm).
  3. Gravity: Leave 1m at the top for water weight.
  4. The U-Turn: Ensure the outside pipe is higher than the sand so the "Living Scum" doesn't dry out.
  5. Ripening: Wait 10-14 days until the water comes out fresh and clear.

Vocabulary of the Foundation

  • Schmutzdecke: The biological film of "good germs" that eats the pathogens in the sand.
  • Adsorption: How biochar attracts and holds onto toxins on its surface.
  • Pathogen: A microscopic organism that causes disease.
  • Manifold: The collection pipes at the bottom of the filter.
