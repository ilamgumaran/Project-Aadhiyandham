Orienting: Where Am I? (Step-by-Step)

1. Identifying Your Surroundings

Before moving, you must anchor yourself to the land. Follow these steps to find your exact location.

Step 1: Finding Your Sentinels

Pick two landmarks (A and B) that you can see clearly. They should be far apart from each other.

  • Validation Check: Hold your arms out toward both. If the angle is wider than 45°, they are good sentinels.

Visual Triangulation: The 3-Point Check

When three lines from three different landmarks meet at one spot, your position is 100% verified.

Step 2: The Water-Flow Check

Look at the nearest river or stream. Water always flows downhill.

  • Validation Check: If water flows toward your left, and your map shows a river on the right flowing south, you are facing the wrong way! Turn around.

Step 3: Horizon Matching

Compare the "wiggly lines" (contours) on your map to the mountains you see.

  • Validation Check: If the map shows three peaks and you see only one large plateau, you are likely in the wrong valley. Trust your eyes over your assumptions.

🚀 Practical Implementation Guide for Beginners: The Journey

1. Step-by-Step Action Plan

  • Preparation (Months 1-3): Begin physical conditioning. Walk 5 miles a day with a 20lb backpack. Acquire baseplate compasses and physical topographical maps.
  • Execution (Transit): Never travel at night unless necessary. Establish a buddy system.
  • Arrival: When you reach the candidate location, establish a temporary perimeter before resting.

2. Troubleshooting & Failure Modes

  • Lost Navigation: If you lose your bearings, STOP. Do not wander. Use the shadow-stick method to find North and re-orient using a known landmark.
  • Injury: Have a designated medic. Prioritize stopping bleeding and stabilizing fractures before continuing.

3. Community & Decadal Flourishing

  • Ensure every adult and capable child knows how to read the map and use the compass. Redundancy is survival.

Vocabulary of the Foundation

Triangulation
A way of determining something's location using the locations of other things.
