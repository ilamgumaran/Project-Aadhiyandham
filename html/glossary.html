Glossary of Terms

This page provides detailed definitions of the core concepts, technologies, and biological terms used throughout the Aadhiyandham manual.

Aadhiyandham (ஆதியந்தம்)
A Tamil portmanteau of Aadhi (Primordial Beginning) and Andham (Final End). It signifies the totality of existence and the cyclic rhythm of the universe.
Biochar
A highly porous form of charcoal produced through pyrolysis. Used as a "biological reef" for soil fertility and a chemical filter for water.
Bloomery
A type of furnace used for smelting iron from its oxides. It produces a mass of wrought iron called a "bloom."
Centered Null
The psychological baseline of Aadhiyandham. A state of emotional stability free from ambition or despair.
E-Prime
English without the verb "to be." Used to eliminate absolute judgments and reduce social conflict.
Glymphatic System
The brain's waste clearance system that flushes metabolic toxins during deep sleep.
Hydraulic Ram Pump (Hydram)
A zero-energy pump that uses the "water hammer" effect to lift water uphill.
Liebig’s Law
The principle that success is determined by the scarcest resource (the limiting factor).
Recycling Cliff
The point when old-world salvage becomes too corroded for use (est. 15-25 years).
Refugia
Geographical areas providing stability during climate or systemic shifts.
Schmutzdecke
The biological layer in a Slow Sand Filter that consumes pathogens.
Terra Preta
Amazonian Dark Earth; soil made regenerative through biochar and compost activation.

🚀 Practical Implementation Guide for Beginners

1. Step-by-Step Action Plan

  • Preparation: Read this module fully before attempting to build or implement. Gather raw materials locally.
  • Execution: Build a small-scale prototype first to test physics and material strength.
  • Review: Test the implementation under stress (wind, water, heavy use) and refine.

2. Troubleshooting & Failure Modes

  • System Failure: When a system breaks, apply the "5 Whys" technique to find the root cause. Fix the root cause, not just the symptom.
  • Burnout: Decadal survival is a marathon. If exhausted, focus on rest and social cohesion.

3. Community & Decadal Flourishing

  • Apprenticeship: No critical skill should be held by only one person. Document local modifications in a physical journal.

Vocabulary of the Foundation

E-Prime
A version of English that excludes the verb 'to be' to focus on objective actions.
Wrought Iron
A tough, malleable form of iron suitable for forging and rolling, rather than casting.
