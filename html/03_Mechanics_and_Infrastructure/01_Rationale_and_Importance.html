Rationale and Importance: Mechanics and Infrastructure

The ability to maintain tools and synthesize materials is the difference between a temporary refugee camp and a permanent civilization. This module focuses on "High-Logic, Low-Tech" engineering.

Why These Steps are Taken

1. Mechanical Advantage (Line-Shafts)

Importance: Energy independence and repairability.

Rationale: Electric motors are difficult to repair. By using water wheels to power a line-shaft, we distribute torque mechanically, ensuring a robust and visible system.

2. Primary Smelting (The Bloomery)

Importance: Surviving the "Recycling Cliff."

Rationale: Scavenged steel will eventually rust. A civilization that cannot produce its own iron cannot replace its tools.

3. Material Synthesis (Chemistry)

Importance: Vertical integration of resources.

Rationale: We must create our own cleaners and preservatives (lye, tannins) to maintain health and durability without global supply chains.

4. Low-Tech Mobility

Importance: Logistical sovereignty.

Rationale: By designing cargo vehicles that utilize human and animal power, we ensure the permanent movement of goods across the settlement.
