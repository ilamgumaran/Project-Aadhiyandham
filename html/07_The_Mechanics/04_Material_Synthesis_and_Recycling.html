1. Turning Rocks into Tools

The Bloomery Furnace is the only technology that can turn raw ore into the iron needed to survive the "Recycling Cliff."

DANGER: SAFETY FIRST

  • Retinal Safety: 1200°C light can damage your retinas. Do not stare directly into the air-hole (tuyere).
  • Skin Protection: Wear thick leather aprons and face shields. Molten slag is like liquid glass.
  • Buddy System: Never smelt alone. If someone faints from the heat, they need immediate rescue.

Anatomy of a Smelt: The Bloomery Furnace

The furnace uses high-carbon monoxide to "strip" oxygen from the ore, leaving a spongy mass of iron at the base.

Step-by-Step Operation

  1. Roast: Heat raw ore rocks until red-hot, then throw into cold water. This removes moisture and makes them easy to grind.
  2. Charge: Add charcoal and ore in a 1:1 ratio.
  3. Blast: Pump bellows for 6-8 hours to maintain 1200°C.
  4. Extract: Break the front arch and pull out the spongy iron bloom.
  5. Shingle: Immediately hammer the bloom to squeeze out liquid slag.

Vocabulary of the Foundation

  • Tuyere: The pipe carrying the air blast into the furnace.
  • Slag: Glassy waste material from smelting iron.
  • Shingling: Hammering a hot iron bloom to remove slag.
  • Reduction: The chemical process of removing oxygen from ore.
  • Refractory: Heat-resistant material used for furnace linings.
