Bio-Security: Water Purification (Slow Sand Filter)

1. Why This Is Important

A Slow Sand Filter (SSF) uses a biological layer called a Schmutzdecke to consume pathogens. It requires zero electricity and zero chemical refills.

2. Expected Outcome

2,000 Liters of safe water per day for a group of 50 people.

Slow Sand Filter Construction

Step-by-Step Assembly

  1. Internal Manifold: Place perforated pipe at the bottom.
  2. Gravel (30cm): Layer larger stones, then pea-gravel.
  3. Biochar (20cm): Add crushed hardwood charcoal.
  4. Fine Sand (80cm): Level washed masonry sand.
  5. External Riser: Ensure outlet is higher than the sand level to keep the bed submerged.
  6. Ripening: Wait 10-14 days for the bio-layer to form before drinking.
