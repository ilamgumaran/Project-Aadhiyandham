# Solar and Thermal Balance: Site Management Guide

## 1. Why This Is Important
Human biology, plant health, and energy sovereignty all depend on the sun. Most modern civilizations solve the problem of cold or darkness by burning external fuel (coal, wood, gas). In Aadhiyandham, we solve it through **Passive Solar Design**. By understanding the specific biological "dosage" humans need and orienting our structures to catch the sun's rhythm, we eliminate the need for high-energy inputs.

## 2. Expected Outcome
A settlement where occupants maintain high serotonin and Vitamin D levels naturally, and where buildings require 75% less fuel for heating during winter.

## 4. Solar Orientation Instructions (Step-by-Step)

### Step 1: Mapping the Solar Path
1.  Identify **True South** (Northern Hemisphere) or **True North** (Southern Hemisphere).
2.  Using a stick or a compass, map the sun's highest point at Midday and its lowest point during the Winter Solstice.
3.  **Observation:** Are there mountain peaks or large trees blocking the sun during the hours of 10:00 AM to 2:00 PM in winter?
4.  **Action:** If yes, move your building site higher up the slope until you clear the "Solar Obstruction Line."

### Step 2: Designing for the Biological Dose
1.  **Morning Sanctuary:** Design your primary sleeping quarters or "Morning Common Area" with large east-facing windows.
2.  **Protocol:** Ensure every member of the group spends the first 30 minutes after sunrise in this zone. 
3.  **Outcome:** This triggers the "Serotonin Spike," providing mental clarity and energy for the day's labor.

### Step 3: Passive Solar Heating (The Thermal Buffer)
1.  **Structure Orientation:** Align the long axis of your buildings East-West.
2.  **The Window Wall:** Place 80% of your windows on the side facing the Equator (South in the North, North in the South).
3.  **Thermal Mass:** Place heavy materials (Rammed Earth, Stone, or Water Barrels) directly in the path of the sunlight entering these windows.
4.  **Mechanism:** The mass absorbs heat during the day and slowly releases it at night, maintaining a stable temperature without a fire.

### Step 4: Midday UVB Protocol
1.  Identify a "Sun Deck" or clear area protected from the wind.
2.  **Schedule:** Between 11:00 AM and 1:00 PM, settlers should expose skin (arms/face) to direct sun for 15-20 minutes.
3.  **Caution:** Do not exceed 30 minutes without shade to prevent UV damage.

## 6. Maintenance of the Balance
*   **Glazing:** Keep windows clean of dust and soot to maximize lux penetration.
*   **Pruning:** Periodically thin the trees to the Equator-side of the settlement to ensure the "Solar Obstruction Line" does not rise over time.
