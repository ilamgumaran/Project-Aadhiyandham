# Initial Site Layout: The 6 Zones of Permaculture

## 1. Why This Is Important
Without a logical layout, a settlement wastes 40% of its caloric energy on inefficient movement. By organizing the site into **Zones**, we ensure that high-maintenance elements (like water filters and kitchens) are close to the center, while low-maintenance elements (like timber forests) are on the periphery.

## 2. The Zoning System (Step-by-Step)

### Zone 0: The Home (Center)
*   **Focus:** Sleeping, recovery, and internal grounding.
*   **Architecture:** Follow the Pattern Language (Module 05).

### Zone 1: Immediate Surroundings (Daily)
*   **Focus:** Highest maintenance.
*   **Elements:** **Slow Sand Filter**, Nursery, Composting Latrines, Herb Garden.
*   **Distance:** Within 20 meters of Zone 0.

### Zone 2: Semi-Intense Cultivation (Every 2 Days)
*   **Focus:** Perennial food sources.
*   **Elements:** Main vegetable beds, Small fruit trees, Poultry.

### Zone 3: The Farm (Weekly)
*   **Focus:** Staple crops and large animals.
*   **Elements:** **Top 10 Staples** (Potatoes/Corn), Pastures for Oxen/Mules.

### Zone 4: Semi-Wild (Seasonal)
*   **Focus:** Resource extraction.
*   **Elements:** Managed timber for the **Bloomery**, foraging grounds.

### Zone 5: Wilderness (Never)
*   **Focus:** Observation and ecological restoration.
*   **Rule:** No permanent structures. Use only for the "Daily Stillness" (Module 03).

## 3. Sector Analysis
Before building, map the **External Energies** moving through the site:
1.  **Sun Path:** (Module 02.03) Orient for winter warmth.
2.  **Prevailing Wind:** (Module 02.02) Use ridges or Zone 4 forests as buffers.
3.  **Water Flow:** Identify the "Gravity Line" from your source to Zone 1.
